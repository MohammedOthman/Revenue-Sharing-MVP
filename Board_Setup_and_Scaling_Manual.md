# Partner Revenue OS — Board Setup & Scaling Manual
## How to Build, Run, and Scale a Board for an Early-Stage Company — and Reach Board Readiness Before Scaling Up

---

## 0. Purpose of This MD

This document is the **Board Setup & Scaling Manual** for the company building **Partner Revenue OS** (commercial brand: **Reven**).

It answers four questions in plain English:

1. **Do we even need a board yet** — and what is the difference between a board, an advisory board, and informal mentors?
2. **How do we set one up correctly** — seats, composition, legal documents, equity, and onboarding?
3. **How do we run it** so it creates value instead of consuming founder time?
4. **How do we know we are board-ready before we scale up** — the readiness gate that must be passed before the company adds capital, headcount, and a real fiduciary board.

It also defines **what we will need to build into our own product** so that Reven can eventually help *its customers* run partner-revenue governance with the same board-grade discipline we apply to ourselves (see §13).

> **Phase discipline (governing):** This manual operates inside the canonical roadmap **Phase 1 Capture (PRM) → Phase 2 Settle → Phase 3 Orchestrate** (source of truth: `Reverse_Engineered_Strategy_PRM_to_Revenue_Sharing_to_Orchestration.md`; concordance in `ROADMAP_ALIGNMENT_AUDIT.md` §2). Board structure scales **with** the phase: a lightweight advisory board in Phase 1 (pre-seed Capture), a real fiduciary board at the seed round, committees and independents only as the company crosses into Phase 2 Settle, where it begins to touch money movement and therefore real fiduciary and compliance risk. **Do not over-build the board ahead of the phase.** A 5-seat fiduciary board with committees at pre-seed is governance theatre that slows a company that has not yet earned its Phase-1 exit gate.

> **Scope & disclaimer:** This is an internal operating playbook, not legal advice. Every share issuance, board appointment, and governance document must be reviewed by qualified corporate counsel in the company's jurisdiction(s) — for Reven, that means **KSA (Saudi Arabia)** company law and, if the holding entity sits offshore, the relevant ADGM / DIFC / Cayman / Delaware framework. See §11.

# 1. Board Philosophy for an Early-Stage Company

## 1.1 A board is a control system, not a status symbol

The same logic Reven sells to customers — *clarity, accountability, evidence, cadence, and control* — is what a board provides to a company. A board exists to do four things and nothing else:

| Board function | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|
| **Govern** | Hold the founder/CEO accountable to the company and its shareholders; approve the things only owners can approve. |
| **Decide** | Make or ratify the small number of decisions that are too big for the CEO alone (budget, raise, M&A, hiring/firing the CEO, option pool). |
| **Support** | Open doors, recruit, advise on strategy, lend credibility to fundraising and enterprise sales. |
| **Protect** | Ensure fiduciary duty, compliance, and risk management — especially critical once Reven moves toward money movement in Phase 2 Settle. |

If a proposed board member or board activity does not serve one of these four functions, it does not belong on the board's agenda.

## 1.2 Match the board to the stage — never run ahead of the phase

The single most common early-stage mistake is building a heavy board too early. Governance has a cost: founder time, legal fees, slower decisions, and diluted control. That cost is only worth paying when the company has crossed the threshold where the board's value exceeds its drag.

```text
Wrong:  Pre-seed startup with 0 paying customers builds a 5-person fiduciary
        board with an audit committee. → governance theatre, slow, demoralizing.

Right:  Board weight grows with phase, capital raised, and risk taken on.
        Advisory in Phase 1 → fiduciary at seed → committees at Phase 2 Settle.
```

## 1.3 The three things a board is NOT

- A board is **not management.** It governs the CEO; it does not run the company day-to-day.
- A board is **not free consulting.** Advisors give advice with no fiduciary duty; board members owe legal duties to the company.
- A board is **not a rubber stamp.** A board that only nods is a wasted seat and a governance risk.

# 2. The Vocabulary — Get These Distinctions Right First

Founders routinely conflate these. Setting up the wrong instrument creates legal and equity problems that are expensive to unwind.

| Instrument | Fiduciary duty? | Typical comp | When Reven uses it | Binding power |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mentor / informal advisor** | No | None / coffee | Day 1 | None — advice only |
| **Advisory board member** | No (advisory only) | 0.1–0.5% equity, vesting | Phase 1 (pre-seed) | None — advice only |
| **Board observer** | No (but sees board materials) | None (usually an investor right) | Seed onward | Attends, no vote |
| **Board director (fiduciary)** | **Yes** — duty of care + loyalty | Equity (independents); investors usually uncompensated | Seed round onward | **Votes** on board matters |
| **Board committee member** | Yes (subset of directors) | Within director comp | Phase 2 Settle / Series A+ | Votes within committee remit |

**Rule:** Until the seed round, Reven runs an **advisory board** (no fiduciary duties, no votes) plus the founder(s). A true voting fiduciary board is created **at the priced/SAFE-converting round** when investors take a seat — see §4.

# 3. Board Evolution by Phase — The Reven Map

This is the spine of the manual. Each phase has a target board shape, who sits on it, and the readiness gate that must be passed before moving up.

## 3.1 Phase 1 — Capture (PRM) · ~Months 0–9 · Pre-seed

**Board shape:** No fiduciary board. **Founder(s) as sole director(s) + a 2–4 person advisory board.**

| Element | Target |
|---|---|
| Voting directors | Founder(s) only (1–2 seats) |
| Advisory board | 2–4 advisors, each on a vesting advisory agreement (FAST-style) |
| Cadence | Advisors: ad-hoc + one monthly group call. No formal board meetings yet. |
| Equity cost | 0.1–0.5% per advisor, 1–2 yr vesting, single trigger acceleration |
| What advisors give | Enterprise/partner-revenue domain credibility, design-partner intros, KSA market access, fundraising warm intros |

**Who to recruit in Phase 1 (priority order):**
1. A **partner-revenue / channel / RevOps operator** from a B2B company who has lived the pain Reven solves.
2. A **KSA enterprise door-opener** — someone who can get Reven into the 3–5 design partners needed for the Phase-1 exit gate (see `Saudi_Value_Pool_and_ICP_Strategic_Analysis.md`).
3. A **fintech/payments or compliance advisor** — *not* active yet, but recruited early so they are ready when Phase 2 Settle introduces money-movement risk.
4. Optionally a **founder-coach / repeat founder** for the CEO.

## 3.2 Phase 2 — Settle (revenue system-of-record) · ~Months 9–24 · Seed → Series A

**Board shape:** First **real fiduciary board.** Typically **3 seats**, expanding toward 5.

| Element | Target (seed) | Target (Series A) |
|---|---|---|
| Composition | 2 founders/common + 1 investor | 2 common + 2 investor + 1 independent |
| Cadence | Quarterly formal board meetings + monthly investor update | Monthly or every 6 weeks |
| Committees | None yet | Begin **Audit** committee as money movement nears |
| Key reason to formalize | Phase 2 introduces the append-only ledger, settlement, and compliance engine — **real fiduciary and regulatory risk** | Scaling capital and headcount |

**Critical phase-linked rule:** Reven does **not** move money before the Phase-2 settlement gate (idempotent settlement, clean ERP reconciliation, CFO reference trust). The board — specifically a fiduciary board with a compliance-literate independent and the beginnings of an audit function — **must be in place before** any settlement/disbursement capability is operated. Do not build the money rail with only a founder-controlled board.

## 3.3 Phase 3 — Orchestrate (partnership network) · Months 24+ · Series A/B+

**Board shape:** Mature **5–7 seat** board with formal committees.

| Element | Target |
|---|---|
| Composition | Balanced common / investor / **2+ independents** |
| Committees | **Audit**, **Compensation**, and (given money movement on the network) a **Risk/Compliance** committee |
| Cadence | Every 6–8 weeks + committee meetings between board meetings |
| Independents | Bring sector gravitas: payments/fintech, large-enterprise GTM, regulatory |

# 4. Board Composition — How to Get the Math Right

## 4.1 The board-size rule

Keep the board **odd-numbered and small** for as long as possible. Effective early boards are **3, then 5, then 7**. Even-numbered boards risk deadlock; large boards (9+) stop being working bodies.

## 4.2 The control arithmetic founders must protect

Every board seat is a slice of control. Founders should map control deliberately at each round.

```text
Seed (3 seats):     2 Common (founders)  + 1 Investor          → founders retain majority
Series A (5 seats): 2 Common + 2 Investor + 1 Independent      → balance; independent is swing
Series B (7 seats): 2 Common + 3 Investor + 2 Independent      → negotiate independent selection
```

**Founder protections to negotiate (with counsel):**
- The **independent seat selection** should require **mutual consent** of common and preferred — never investor-appointed alone.
- Define **protective provisions** (what needs board / preferred approval) tightly in the financing docs.
- Keep an **expandable board** clause so future rounds add seats without renegotiating the whole structure.

## 4.3 The skills matrix — recruit for gaps, not for friends

Before adding any seat, score the current board against the skills the *next 18 months* require. Recruit to fill the **red** cells, not to add another version of the founder.

| Capability | Why Reven needs it | Phase most critical |
|---|---|---|
| Partner-revenue / channel / RevOps domain | Core product credibility | 1 |
| Enterprise B2B GTM (KSA + GCC) | Land design partners and first ARR | 1–2 |
| Payments / fintech / money movement | Phase 2 settlement & licensing strategy | 2 |
| Compliance / regulatory (KSA SAMA-aware) | Money movement = regulated activity | 2–3 |
| Finance / fundraising / CFO-grade | Burn, runway, next raise | all |
| Scaling / org-building | Going from 10 → 50+ people | 3 |
| Repeat founder / CEO coaching | Founder support | all |

# 5. Sourcing & Recruiting Board Members

## 5.1 Where they come from

| Source | Best for |
|---|---|
| Existing investors | Investor board seats (come with the round) |
| Design partners' executives | Domain credibility (recruit as advisors first) |
| Warm intros from investors/advisors | Independents — investors have networks built for this |
| Operators you already admire | Advisory board, later independents |
| Specialist board-placement networks | Independents at Series A+ |

## 5.2 The "date before you marry" rule

**Never** put someone straight onto a fiduciary board. The progression is:

```text
Mentor / 1 conversation
   → Advisor (vesting agreement, 3–6 months working together)
      → Observer (sees how the board runs)
         → Director (fiduciary seat) — only after proven value + fit
```

This protects against the most expensive early-stage mistake: a bad board member is far harder to remove than a bad advisor.

## 5.3 Diligence checklist before offering any seat

- Reference checks with founders they have served (ask: *"Would you put them on your board again?"*).
- Conflict check: do they advise/sit on a competitor or a payments rail Reven might partner or compete with?
- Bandwidth check: how many boards do they already sit on? (5+ is a red flag for an active seat.)
- Values/behavior check: how do they behave when a portfolio company is in trouble?
- KSA/region fit: do they understand the regulatory and commercial environment Reven operates in?

# 6. Onboarding a New Board Member

A board member who is not onboarded is a board member who slows the board down. Run a **structured 30-day onboarding**.

| When | What |
|---|---|
| Before first meeting | Send the **board handbook**: cap table, financing docs, last 3 board decks, current strategy dossier (`Partner_Revenue_OS_Master_Strategy_Dossier.md`), the phase model, KPI definitions, and this manual. |
| Day 0 | Sign **director agreement / advisory agreement**, D&O insurance confirmation, confidentiality, conflict-of-interest disclosure. |
| Week 1 | 1:1 with CEO on strategy + 1:1 with CFO/finance on the model (`Pre_Seed_2M_SAR_Financial_Model_Blueprint.md`). |
| Week 2 | Product walkthrough of the Reven platform; meet key team leads. |
| First meeting | Clear statement of what the company needs from this person (their "charter"). |

# 7. Board Operating Cadence — Make Meetings Worth Everyone's Time

This section plugs into the company's broader rhythm defined in `Internal_Operating_Cadence_Manual.md`. The board cadence is the **top layer** of that cadence.

## 7.1 Meeting rhythm by phase

| Phase | Formal board | Investor/advisor update | Committee meetings |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (pre-seed) | None (advisory call monthly) | Monthly written update | — |
| 2 (seed→A) | Quarterly → every 6 weeks | Monthly written update | Audit forming |
| 3 (A/B+) | Every 6–8 weeks | Monthly written update | Audit, Comp, Risk between meetings |

## 7.2 The "no surprises" rule

The board pack goes out **at least 48–72 hours before** the meeting and is **read in advance**. Meetings are for **discussion and decisions**, not for the CEO presenting slides line-by-line. Bad news goes to the board **early and directly**, never first revealed in the room.

## 7.3 Standard board-meeting agenda (90 minutes)

```text
1. Approve prior minutes + consent agenda           (5 min)   [vote]
2. CEO summary: top 3 wins, top 3 risks, 1 ask      (10 min)
3. Metrics & finance review vs plan                  (20 min)  → ties to Monthly CFO Review
4. Deep-dive: ONE strategic topic                    (30 min)  → e.g. Phase-2 settlement gate readiness
5. Decisions requiring board approval                (15 min)  [votes]
6. Closed session (directors only, no management)    (10 min)
```

## 7.4 The board pack — what goes in it

Reuse the CFO review pack (`Monthly_CFO_Review_Manual.md`) plus governance items:

- One-page CEO letter (wins / risks / asks).
- Metrics vs plan: the Phase-appropriate proof metrics (Capture: claims registered, design partners, weekly active usage; Settle: settled flow, reconciliation accuracy, dispute resolution time).
- Financials: cash, burn, runway, vs budget.
- Hiring plan and org updates.
- Pipeline / GTM (from `GTM_Operating_Manual.md`).
- Risks & mitigations register.
- Specific decisions requested, each with a recommendation.
- Appendix: detailed financials, product roadmap.

# 8. Decisions That Require the Board

Define this explicitly in the bylaws/financing docs so neither founder nor board oversteps.

| Decision class | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Capital** | New financing round, debt, valuation, option pool increase |
| **People** | Hiring/firing the CEO, C-suite comp, equity grants above threshold |
| **Strategic** | M&A, entering money movement (Phase 2 gate), major pivots |
| **Financial** | Annual budget approval, spend above authority limit |
| **Governance** | Adding/removing board seats, bylaw changes, related-party transactions |

Everything else is the CEO's to run. A board that reaches below this line into operations is a dysfunctional board.

---

# 9. Board Committees — Only as You Scale

Committees are a Phase 2/3 instrument. Do not create them at pre-seed.

| Committee | Forms when | Remit |
|---|---|---|
| **Audit** | Approaching Phase 2 money movement / Series A | Financial controls, external audit, the append-only ledger integrity, compliance reporting |
| **Compensation** | Series A+ | CEO/exec comp, option pool, equity policy |
| **Risk / Compliance** | Phase 2–3 (money on the network) | Regulatory posture (KSA SAMA & relevant rails), AML/KYC, settlement risk |

**Reven-specific:** because the product itself moves toward being a revenue system-of-record and later a settlement rail, the **Audit and Risk committees matter earlier and more** than for a typical SaaS company. Stand up the audit function in step with the Phase-2 settlement build, not after.

# 10. Equity & Compensation for Board Members

| Role | Typical comp | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Advisor (Phase 1) | 0.1–0.5% equity, 1–2 yr vest, monthly | Use a standard advisory agreement (FAST-style); reserve from a small advisor pool. |
| Investor director | **Usually none** | They are compensated through their investment; do not grant extra equity. |
| Independent director | 0.25–1.0% equity, vesting over service; sometimes cash at later stages | Negotiated; lower end early-stage. |
| Committee chairs | Within director grant | Cash retainers appear later (Series B+). |
| All directors | **D&O insurance + expense reimbursement** | D&O is non-negotiable before recruiting independents. |

**Rule:** keep a dedicated, ring-fenced **advisor/independent equity pool** (commonly 1–2% total) so board comp does not erode the employee option pool or surprise the cap table.

# 11. Legal & Governance Foundations (KSA-Aware)

Set these up *before* you need them. Engage corporate counsel for all of it.

| Document / item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Articles / bylaws | Board size, voting, quorum, how directors are appointed/removed |
| Shareholders' agreement | Board seat rights, protective provisions, drag/tag, transfer restrictions |
| Director appointment letters | Each director's duties, term, confidentiality, IP, conflict policy |
| Advisory agreements | Advisor scope, equity, vesting, termination |
| D&O insurance | Protects directors personally — required before independents will join |
| Conflict-of-interest policy | Disclosure and recusal rules |
| Board minutes & resolutions | Legal record of every decision — keep them disciplined from day one |
| Cap table (single source of truth) | Tracks every seat's equity; keep it clean |

**KSA / jurisdiction notes (verify with counsel):**
- Confirm the **legal entity structure** — a KSA operating company, and whether a holding entity (e.g., ADGM/DIFC or offshore) is used for the cap table and investor seats.
- Saudi company law governs **board formation, director duties, and shareholder rights** for the local entity; align bylaws accordingly.
- For Phase 2 money movement, board-level oversight must anticipate **SAMA** (Saudi Central Bank) regulatory expectations for payments/settlement activity — this is *why* the compliance independent and audit/risk committees are sequenced into Phase 2.
- Keep board minutes and resolutions in a form acceptable to both local regulators and future foreign investors.

# 12. Board Readiness Gate — The Checklist Before You Scale Up

This is the heart of the request: **readiness before scaling up.** Do not raise a larger round, take on a fiduciary board, or build the money rail until the company can answer **yes** to every item below. Treat it as a gate, the same way the phase model gates capability.

## 12.1 Governance readiness

- [ ] Legal entity and cap table are clean and current (single source of truth).
- [ ] Bylaws/shareholders' agreement define board size, seats, protective provisions, and the independent-selection mechanism.
- [ ] D&O insurance is in place (before recruiting independents).
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest and confidentiality policies exist and are signed.
- [ ] A disciplined practice of minutes and board resolutions already exists from the advisory period.

## 12.2 Operating readiness

- [ ] Monthly investor/board update has been running reliably (proves reporting muscle) — see `Monthly_CFO_Review_Manual.md`.
- [ ] Metrics are defined, instrumented, and trusted (the board pack can be produced in < 1 day).
- [ ] Financial model and budget exist and actuals are tracked against them (`Pre_Seed_2M_SAR_Financial_Model_Blueprint.md`).
- [ ] Internal operating cadence is functioning (`Internal_Operating_Cadence_Manual.md`) — the board is the top layer, not the only layer.

## 12.3 Phase readiness (the linkage that matters most for Reven)

- [ ] **Phase-1 exit gate met before formalizing a fiduciary board for scale:** 100+ real claims, 3–5 design partners with finance-accepted evidence packs, weekly active usage.
- [ ] **Before any Phase-2 money movement:** fiduciary board seated, compliance-literate independent recruited, audit function forming, settlement gate criteria defined (idempotent settlement, clean ERP reconciliation, CFO reference trust).
- [ ] Skills-matrix gaps for the *next* phase are identified and a recruiting plan exists for them.

## 12.4 Founder readiness

- [ ] Founder(s) understand they are gaining a boss (the board can replace the CEO) and have negotiated control protections deliberately.
- [ ] Founder has practiced "no surprises" reporting during the advisory phase.
- [ ] There is a clear separation in the founder's mind between **governing (board)** and **running (management)**.

> **Gate rule:** If a row is unchecked, the action it gates does not happen. A board built before this gate is passed will slow the company; a money rail built before the Phase-2 governance items are in place is a regulatory and fiduciary risk.

# 13. What This Means For Our Product (Reven)

The user's ask — *"an end-to-end guideline to what we will need to do in our product"* — has a second reading: Reven's customers are B2B entities that themselves need **governance-grade control** over partner revenue. The discipline in this manual maps directly onto product capability we should build, **phase-aligned**:

| Manual concept | Product capability in Reven | Reven phase |
|---|---|---|
| Board pack / evidence in advance | **Evidence archive + executive reporting** — exportable, board-ready partner-revenue packs | 1 Capture |
| "No surprises" / audit trail | **Append-only audit log** of every claim, attribution, and decision | 1→2 |
| Decisions requiring approval | **Approval / dispute workflow** with role-based authority limits | 1→2 |
| Audit & risk committee oversight | **Compliance engine + reconciliation reporting** for the system-of-record | 2 Settle |
| Metrics the board trusts | **KPI module** with defined, instrumented partner-revenue metrics | 1→3 |
| Governance documents | **Legal-document / agreement-metadata module** (contracts, terms, evidence) | 1→2 |
| Protective provisions / control | **Protection windows + attribution rules** as the customer's control layer | 1 |

**Build sequencing (do not run ahead of the platform's phase):**
1. **Phase 1 (Capture):** ship the evidence archive, audit log, KPI definitions, and executive/board-ready reporting export. This is what lets a customer's *own* board and finance team trust partner-revenue numbers.
2. **Phase 2 (Settle):** add the compliance engine, reconciliation reporting, and approval-authority controls that an audit committee would demand before money moves.
3. **Phase 3 (Orchestrate):** surface partner-P&L and network-level governance reporting suitable for cross-tenant, board-level oversight.

Concrete near-term product backlog items implied by this manual (Phase-1 safe — no money movement):
- A **"Board Pack Export"** that bundles current partner-revenue KPIs, evidence, and audit-log highlights into a single shareable report.
- **Role-based approval thresholds** in the claim/dispute workflow (mirrors §8 decision-authority limits).
- A **governance/metrics dashboard** that maps to the Phase-appropriate proof metrics in §7.4.

> These tie to the existing modules in `revenue-share-platform/` (partner, contract, revenue, KPI, and legal-document modules). Treat the rows above as the bridge between this governance manual and the product roadmap in `Partner_Revenue_OS_PDR.md` / `partner-revenue-os-PDR-v5.md`.

# 14. Common Mistakes & Red Flags

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Building a fiduciary board at pre-seed | Governance theatre, slows decisions | Run an advisory board in Phase 1 |
| Giving a board seat to anyone who asks | Hard to remove a bad director | "Date before you marry" (§5.2) |
| Even-numbered or oversized board | Deadlock / not a working body | Keep it 3 → 5 → 7, odd |
| Investor controls the independent seat | Founder loses balance of power | Mutual-consent selection (§4.2) |
| Surprising the board with bad news | Destroys trust, triggers intervention | "No surprises" rule (§7.2) |
| No D&O insurance | Can't recruit independents; personal risk | Put D&O in place first (§10) |
| Board reaching into operations | Demoralizes team, confuses authority | Define decision rights (§8) |
| Building the money rail before governance | Regulatory + fiduciary exposure | Phase-2 readiness gate (§12.3) |
| Over-granting board equity | Erodes cap table / employee pool | Ring-fenced advisor pool (§10) |
