# 1. Executive GTM Doctrine

## 1.1 The Market Problem

When partnerships are small, companies can manage them through relationships, spreadsheets, CRM notes, email, WhatsApp, and manual follow-up.

When partnerships become commercially material, this breaks.

The customer begins to suffer from:

- unclear partner attribution,
- sales and partnership conflict,
- duplicate claims,
- weak partner protection,
- partner distrust,
- spreadsheet payout logic,
- unclear agreement applicability,
- finance uncertainty,
- hidden payout exposure,
- weak executive visibility,
- manual reporting,
- and lack of confidence in partner ROI.

## 1.2 The Product Category

The company should not say:

```text
We are a partner portal.
We are a PRM.
We are a commission tool.
We are a dashboard.
```

The company should say:

```text
We help companies run partner revenue like a controlled revenue line.
```

## 1.3 The GTM Doctrine

GTM must create four forms of trust.

| Trust type | Meaning | How GTM creates it |
|---|---|---|
| Problem trust | Buyer believes the pain is real and urgent | Education, diagnosis, current-state mapping |
| Vendor trust | Buyer believes the team understands the domain | Founder-led insight, relevant examples, operating fluency |
| Product trust | Buyer believes the product can handle the workflow | Pain-led demos, pilot, implementation |
| Economic trust | Buyer believes the product is worth paying for | ROI case, finance evidence, value review |

## 1.4 The Operator Principle

The company should not measure GTM only by pipeline.

It should measure GTM by whether it moves a customer through operational maturity:

```text
Unaware
→ aware
→ educated
→ diagnosed
→ aligned
→ committed
→ implemented
→ activated
→ value-proven
→ expanded
→ referenceable
```

## Stage 2 — ICP Definition

### Objective

Define the exact type of company that should be pursued first.

### Operator Rationale

ICP must be operational, not generic.

Do not define ICP as “B2B SaaS companies.”

Define it as:

```text
B2B SaaS companies with 20–200 active partners, CRM adoption, manual partner attribution, partner-sourced/influenced revenue, and executive pressure to prove partner ROI.
```

### ICP Dimensions

| Dimension | Strong ICP Signal | Weak ICP Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Partner count | 20–200+ active partners | Fewer than 10 informal partners |
| Partner revenue | Material pipeline/revenue | Partnerships are mostly brand alliances |
| Partner types | Referral, reseller, co-sell, implementation | One simple informal motion |
| Attribution pain | Sales/partner credit conflict | No credit disputes |
| Finance pain | Payout or revenue-share exposure | No financial consequence |
| CRM maturity | CRM exists but partner fields weak | No CRM discipline |
| Executive pressure | CRO/CFO/CEO asks for partner ROI | No leadership interest |
| Budget | Can pay annual SaaS + implementation | No owner or budget |

### ICP Definition Template

```text
Our first ICP is:
[company type] in [segment/geography]
with [partner count]
using [CRM/process maturity]
where partnerships create [revenue/pipeline impact]
but current operations suffer from [specific pain]
and the buyer is [role]
with validation from [CRO/CFO/CEO/RevOps].
```

### Done Means

Every operator can say who we sell to and who we do not sell to.

### Operator Rationale

| Step | Channel | Purpose |

# 11. Failure Modes and Recovery Playbooks

## Failure Mode 1 — Outreach Gets Replies but No Qualified Meetings

### Diagnosis

Messaging may be interesting but not urgent.

### Fix

- Tighten ICP.
- Use more specific pain.
- Add trigger-based outreach.
- Change persona.
- Use stronger problem framing.

---

## Failure Mode 2 — Discovery Calls Are Good but No Diagnostic Workshops

### Diagnosis

Buyer may like the idea but not feel enough urgency.

### Fix

- Quantify pain earlier.
- Ask about current process failures.
- Bring executive angle.
- Offer lightweight diagnostic.
- Use current-state checklist.

---

## Failure Mode 3 — Demos Do Not Convert to Pilots

### Diagnosis

Demo may be feature-led instead of pain-led.

### Fix

- Demo one real workflow.
- Use customer-like data.
- Show before/after.
- Show CFO/CRO value.
- Define pilot success criteria during demo.

---

## Failure Mode 4 — Pilots Do Not Convert

### Diagnosis

Pilot may lack success criteria, executive sponsor, or real usage.

### Fix

- Make pilot paid or commercially committed.
- Define conversion criteria upfront.
- Run weekly pilot review.
- Track activation score.
- Involve economic buyer by mid-pilot.

---

## Failure Mode 5 — Customers Sign but Do Not Activate

### Diagnosis

Implementation, training, or workflow design is weak.

### Fix

- Tighten kickoff.
- Use implementation checklist.
- Reduce scope.
- Launch first workflow faster.
- Add role-based training.
- Assign customer owner.

---

## Failure Mode 6 — Customers Use Product but Executives Do Not See Value

### Diagnosis

Executive dashboards or value reviews are weak.

### Fix

- Create monthly executive pack.
- Quantify claims, time saved, evidence gaps.
- Show payout exposure and partner revenue control.
- Tie usage to business case.

---

## Failure Mode 7 — Implementation Becomes Too Custom

### Diagnosis

Team is accepting too many customer-specific requirements.

### Fix

- Define standard implementation packages.
- Charge for custom work.
- Move non-repeatable items to paid services.
- Reject low-ACV custom requests.
- Create configuration, not customization.

# 13. Launch Governance and Decision Gates

## Gate 1 — ICP Gate

Do not scale outreach until:

- first ICP is defined,
- poor-fit customers are defined,
- account list is scored,
- role-specific pain is clear.

## Gate 2 — Messaging Gate

Do not invest in paid marketing until:

- outbound reply rates show resonance,
- discovery calls confirm pain,
- buyers repeat problem language.

## Gate 3 — Sales Hire Gate

Do not hire AE until:

- founder has closed or nearly closed customers,
- pipeline exceeds founder capacity,
- pricing has been tested,
- sales process is documented.

## Gate 4 — SDR Hire Gate

Do not hire SDR until:

- target list is clear,
- messaging works,
- meetings convert to qualified opportunities,
- follow-up process exists.

## Gate 5 — CS Hire Gate

Do not hire CS too late.

Hire CS when:

- implementation becomes a bottleneck,
- customers need structured onboarding,
- activation is at risk,
- founder/product time is being drained.

## Gate 6 — Integration Gate

Do not build integration unless:

- paid customer requires it,
- scope repeats across customers,
- ACV justifies build cost,
- integration does not replace product focus.

## Gate 7 — Expansion Gate

Do not expand account before:

- activation is strong,
- value review is positive,
- customer sponsor agrees,
- implementation capacity exists.

# 15. The Final Launch-to-Market Operating Principle

Partner Revenue OS does not launch when:

```text
the website is live,
the deck is ready,
or the product demo exists.
```

It launches when:

```text
a real ICP customer understands the pain,
trusts the team,
completes diagnosis,
signs a paid pilot,
implements the workflow,
processes partner revenue claims,
uses the system in operating cadence,
sees measurable value,
expands usage,
and becomes proof for the next customer.
```

The company is not just launching a software product.

It is launching a new operating discipline for partner revenue governance.
