### The five make-or-break levers (each: 1st → 2nd-order)

1. **Reprice partner-count → value/flow before the count S-curve flattens.** → It's our NRR ceiling; the claim-ledger data is what *earns* the right to reprice — moat and pricing power are the same asset.
2. **Compliance = a demand *floor* that buys *endurance*, not a feature.** → Avalara exited at **$8.4B / 27% premium at only 116% NRR** — the market pays for durability, not peak. Endurance is the under-priced top-quartile asset, and compliance is how we buy it.
3. **The module ladder (Capture→Finance→Settlement→P&L) as a forcing function.** → NRR is non-linear by cohort age (Datadog: 84/55/33/18% on 2+/4+/6+/8+ products); cohorts climb slowly then expansion compounds. Measure NRR *by cohort age*.
4. **Geo-compound only on shared-compliance-DNA corridors (GCC).** → Compliance trades cross-border *speed* for in-market *endurance*; reuse the KSA build across the GCC e-invoicing corridor; treat the US/EU leap as a separate funded trough.
5. **A shallow trough / low burn multiple.** → Our SAR cost base makes <1.5×→1.0× burn multiple a structural edge; shallow trough → less dilution → founder control to run the long game. **Hire rep #2 only after rep #1 ramps** (premature scaling kills ~74% of startups).
