## PART 0 — The core idea, in one breath

**One product. One flywheel. Two speeds.**

Reven is **not vertical SaaS** (industry-specific, like Toast-for-restaurants). It is a **horizontal platform built on a single functional wedge — partner revenue & B2B revenue-sharing — that applies across every industry where entities share revenue.** One product can serve a corner shop *and* a sovereign-backed enterprise not because of the industry but because **the value object is identical at every size and in every market: the Partner Revenue Claim.** That makes the right analogs **Stripe, Salesforce, Ramp** — a *universal business function* sold across all industries, self-serve to SMB *and* sales-led to enterprise — **not** Toast. So we keep **one commercial spine** (the same land → prove value → expand → productize flywheel Palantir runs) and run it at **two speeds**:

- **SMB speed — self-serve / product-led (PLG).** The product is the salesperson. Sign up, connect your systems, see value in minutes. Like Crossbeam's free tier, QuotaPath's published $35/seat, Toast's self-onboarding.
- **Enterprise speed — design-partner / sales-led with a pilot.** A human + the product prove value on real data in weeks. Like Impartner, CaptivateIQ, and Palantir's bootcamp-then-pilot.

The two speeds **converge on the same backend** (the Partner Revenue Claim ledger) and feed the **same flywheel**: every enterprise pilot teaches us a pattern → we productize it → the next SMB gets it as a button. That productization discipline is the **anti-FDE move** — we get Palantir's land-and-expand stickiness *without* Palantir's standing army of on-site engineers, because the product is pre-built for this one **function** (partner revenue), not rebuilt per customer.

**Two axes of expansion (the growth model).** Reven grows in two directions at once — this is *why* "horizontal platform, functional wedge" is the right label, not "vertical SaaS":
- **Vertical = depth *within the need*.** Own progressively more of the partner-revenue / revenue-sharing stack: **Capture (PRM) → Settle (revenue system-of-record, ledger, reconciliation, settlement) → Orchestrate (partner P&L, forecasting, multi-company revenue-sharing infrastructure).** Deeper down the money-and-trust chain of the *same* problem (the phase roadmap).
- **Horizontal = breadth *across markets*.** The need isn't industry-bound — it exists wherever B2B entities share revenue: SaaS, fintech, telecom, distribution, channel/manufacturing, professional services, public–private concessions — and across geographies (**KSA → GCC → beyond**). Land in one or two beachhead markets, then broaden.

The flywheel below powers **both** axes: every deeper module *and* every new market reuses the same Partner Revenue Claim core, so neither axis restarts the build.

> **Plain-English north star:** *"Help any company — from a 5-partner reseller to a multi-country ecosystem — run partner revenue like a controlled revenue line, starting in minutes for the small ones and in weeks for the big ones, on one system that gets smarter with every customer."*

## PART 1 — Who we're selling to, and what they actually need (reverse-engineered)

Same product, but the **buyer, the pain, the proof, and the speed differ by segment.** This maps onto Reven's existing 5-tier ladder ([`Reven_Pricing_Executive_Summary.md`](Reven_Pricing_Executive_Summary.md)).

| | **SMB track** (Tiers 1–2: SME, Growth) | **Enterprise track** (Tiers 3–5: Mid-market, Semi-gov, Large) |
|---|---|---|
| **Who signs** | Founder / Head of Partnerships / RevOps — one person decides | A committee: Head of Partnerships *champion* + CFO/Finance *economic buyer* + Legal/Compliance + IT/Security |
| **The pain (plain English)** | "My partner deals live in spreadsheets and WhatsApp; I don't trust who gets paid what." | "Partnerships are now material revenue; I can't prove attribution to finance, disputes are rising, and audit/compliance is exposed." |
| **What they fear** | Setup hassle, getting locked into something heavy, paying for shelfware | Picking the wrong platform, a failed rollout, a security/compliance miss, vendor lock-in |
| **What "value proven" means** | "I connected my CRM and saw my real partner contributions and a clean claim ledger in one afternoon." | "We ran 30–90 days on real data; finance accepted the evidence pack; disputes dropped; attribution is defensible." |
| **Speed they want** | **Minutes to first value**, automated, no calls | **White-glove pilot**, customized, with a human — *self-serve onboarding actively annoys them* ([digitalapplied TTV](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/customer-onboarding-time-to-value-2026-saas-metrics-framework)) |
| **Budget reality** | A new, small line item (≈ one CRM seat). KSA micro-floor ~SAR 50–149/mo | An existing budget line; KSA mid → SAR 188–450K; enterprise/gov SAR 0.6–2.5M; **gov procurement runs 6–12 months** ([upGrowth MENA](https://upgrowth.in/mena-saas-gtm-playbook/)) |
| **Decision driver** | Convenience + trust | Risk-reduction + defensibility + sovereignty (in-Kingdom residency, ZATCA, Sharia) |
| **Their version of "the claim"** | A tidy ledger replacing a spreadsheet | A governed, audit-grade system of record for attribution & eligibility |

**The reverse-engineered insight:** SMBs buy **relief and speed**; enterprises buy **defensibility and control**. The *same* claim ledger delivers both — we just **lead with a different headline and a different speed** per track, and let the product depth (compliance, multi-entity, settlement) reveal itself as they grow. This is why the segments share a spine.

## PART 3 — The unified commercial system (Reven's flywheel: ACQUIRE → ACTIVATE → EXPAND → COMPOUND)

This is Palantir's Acquire→Expand→Scale, adapted to a self-serve+sales hybrid and our phase discipline (**Phase 1 never moves money** — settlement is the Phase-2 expansion conversation).

```
        ┌──────────── THE REVEN FLYWHEEL ────────────┐
        │                                             │
   1. ACQUIRE ──► 2. ACTIVATE ──► 3. CONVERT ──► 4. EXPAND
   (land the      (prove value     (commercial    (partners → modules
    wedge)         on real data)    close)         → compliance → entities
        ▲                                           → settlement volume)
        │                                                   │
        └─────────── 5. COMPOUND (productize + refer) ◄─────┘
              every pilot's learning becomes a product feature,
              every happy customer becomes a reference,
              lowering CAC and time-to-value for the next one
```

**Two tracks through the same flywheel:**
- **SMB:** ACQUIRE = self-serve signup · ACTIVATE = in-product · CONVERT = self-serve upgrade · EXPAND = usage-triggered + sales-assist.
- **Enterprise:** ACQUIRE = design-partner/outbound + "Reven Sprint" (our bootcamp) · ACTIVATE = paid pilot on real data · CONVERT = committee close + fenced implementation · EXPAND = account-managed.

**The compounding engine (the anti-FDE part):** a small **Solutions** function (1–2 people, reporting to **Product, not Sales** — Palantir's lever V3) sits on enterprise pilots, captures every recurring request, and feeds it to product as a **configurable feature**, not a bespoke build. That keeps us SaaS-margin, not services-margin, and means SMBs inherit enterprise-grade capabilities as buttons.

## PART 4 — The GTM playbook, step by step (operational & tactical)

Each stage: **goal · SMB track · Enterprise track · the gate to advance · the metric · benchmark anchor.** Run them as a pipeline.

### STAGE 0 — Segment & target (before any outreach)
- **Goal:** route every lead to the right speed automatically.
- **How:** a 3-question qualifier — (1) # of partners, (2) is finance/legal involved in payouts?, (3) multi-entity/country or compliance mandate? → **< ~25 partners & no finance committee = SMB self-serve; otherwise = Enterprise track.**
- **Gate:** lead tagged SMB or Enterprise with a tier hypothesis (1–5).
- **Metric:** routing accuracy (how often a "SMB" later needs sales, and vice-versa).
- **Benchmark:** PLG-with-sales-assist routes by firmographic + behavioral signal ([growthspree](https://www.growthspreeofficial.com/blogs/plg-product-led-growth-b2b-saas-hybrid-2026)).

### STAGE 1 — ACQUIRE (land the wedge)
- **Goal:** get them *in*, cheaply, with a reason to act now.
- **SMB:** free or **SAR 50/mo** self-serve signup (the published land-wedge); content + community + product-led virality (partners they invite see Reven). *Land-to-expand % is the kill metric — one human support call erases a year's SME revenue.*
- **Enterprise:** outbound to **Head of Partnerships** (champion) with a sharp pain hypothesis; offer a **"Reven Sprint"** — our 1-week bootcamp analog: *"In 5 working days, connect one CRM + one billing system and we'll show your real partner-contribution graph and a clean claim ledger on your own data."* (Palantir's bootcamp compresses an 18-month cycle to 5 days — copy the mechanic.)
- **Gate:** SMB account created **or** Enterprise Sprint scheduled with data access + exec sponsor.
- **Metric:** SMB signups; Enterprise Sprints booked; **founder visibility actions** in KSA (GITEX, local events — GCC buyers weight this heavily).
- **Benchmark:** Crossbeam free tier (SMB); Palantir bootcamp + Impartner outbound (Enterprise).

### STAGE 2 — ACTIVATE (prove value on real data — the make-or-break)
- **Goal:** deliver the **"aha"** fast. This is where most SaaS dies.
- **SMB:** guided in-product onboarding → **first value in minutes** (connect CRM → see contribution graph + claim ledger). Target **time-to-first-value < 1 day**; instrument an activation checklist.
- **Enterprise:** a **30–90 day paid pilot** on real data, scoped to **one high-pain workflow** (claim → attribution → eligibility → finance-evidence pack). White-glove. **AE-led demo + sandbox** beats a bare trial. Deliverable = **finance-accepted evidence pack + measured outcome** (disputes ↓, attribution defensible).
- **Gate:** SMB hits the activation milestone; Enterprise pilot produces a **finance-accepted** value proof.
- **Metric:** **Activation rate** (avg B2B is 37.5%; best-in-class 65–75% — aim there); enterprise pilot **value-proof acceptance**.
- **Benchmark:** TTFV <5 min lifts conversion >25%; faster TTV lifts retention/upsell 20–40% ([digitalapplied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/customer-onboarding-time-to-value-2026-saas-metrics-framework)); enterprise demo+sandbox hybrid ([growthspree benchmarks](https://www.growthspreeofficial.com/blogs/b2b-saas-trial-to-paid-conversion-rate-benchmarks-2026-by-trial-type-acv-length-credit-card)).

### STAGE 3 — CONVERT (commercial close)
- **Goal:** turn proven value into a contract.
- **SMB:** **self-serve upgrade** inside the product (Start → Growth) when they hit a band limit or want statements/disputes. No sales call. Published/"starting-from" pricing.
- **Enterprise:** champion + **CFO** business case (ROI = disputes avoided + finance hours saved + leakage caught); **annual contract above the SAR 95–110K high-touch floor**; **fenced fixed-fee implementation** (priced separately, <20% of revenue). Multi-year for gov with escalation floor + milestone disbursement (gov pays in arrears).
- **Gate:** SMB plan upgraded; Enterprise order signed.
- **Metric:** SMB free/trial → paid %; Enterprise **pilot → paid conversion** (enterprise $75K+ pilots convert ~6–10% opt-in / 26–36% opt-out — so *handpick* pilots and tie them to a committee decision, not a free trial).
- **Benchmark:** Atlassian/DocuSign self-serve upgrade + enterprise sales close; KSA: **SAR pricing + 15% VAT + annual local billing lifts close rates 15–25%** ([upGrowth](https://upgrowth.in/mena-saas-gtm-playbook/)).

### STAGE 4 — ONBOARD / IMPLEMENT (get to live, fast)
- **Goal:** live and trusted, without a 6-month slog.
- **SMB:** **zero-touch / templated** — they're already live from activation. CS is digital/community.
- **Enterprise:** **6–8 week implementation, consultant-free where possible** (Everstage's wedge, *against* CaptivateIQ's 4–6 months). Config, data migration, integrations, Arabic/RTL, compliance accreditation, change management. Push implementation to **local SI partners** over time (and for KSA trust).
- **Gate:** customer in production on the agreed workflow with finance signed off.
- **Metric:** **time-to-live** (target ≤8 weeks enterprise, same-day SMB); implementation as % of revenue (keep <20%).
- **Benchmark:** Everstage 6–8 wks vs CaptivateIQ 4–6 mo vs Spiff 6 mo+ ([Everstage](https://www.everstage.com/commission-software-comparison/spiff-vs-captivateiq)).

### STAGE 5 — EXPAND (the NRR engine — where the money is)
- **Goal:** grow each account along pre-planned dimensions.
- **How (both tracks, sequenced):** more **active-partner bands** → add **modules** (Control → Orchestration) → climb the **compliance ladder** (L1 ZATCA/WHT/PDPL → L2 residency/dedicated tenant → L3 Sharia-certified) → add **entities/countries** → finally, in **Phase 2, the settlement layer** (capped per-payout fee) — the Toast-style "layer fintech, 2–5× revenue/customer" move.
- **Gate:** ≥2–3 live workflows or modules per account; settlement conversation opened only after Phase-2 gate.
- **Metric:** **Net Revenue Retention** — target by segment: **Enterprise ≥118%, Mid ≥108%, SMB protect ≥97%, blended ≥120%**. (Past $10M ARR, 1% NRR > 1% CAC — so this stage is the priority.)
- **Benchmark:** NRR segment benchmarks ([ProductQuant](https://productquant.dev/blog/nrr-benchmarks-saas/)); commission-tool 12–18 month expansion pattern; a16z 2–5× fintech uplift.

### STAGE 6 — COMPOUND (productize + reference — close the loop)
- **Goal:** make the *next* customer cheaper, faster, and more likely.
- **Productize:** the Solutions function turns every recurring pilot request into a **product feature** (reporting to Product, not Sales). Track **"FDE-hours per new deployment trending down"** — i.e., % of new use cases solved by **config vs custom**. This is the anti-FDE flywheel that keeps margins SaaS-grade.
- **Refer:** turn each finance-accepted enterprise win into a **reference + case study** (KSA buyers weight local proof and founder visibility heavily); SMBs refer via in-product partner invites (built-in virality).
- **Gate:** a productized feature shipped from pilot learning; ≥1 referenceable customer per segment.
- **Metric:** config-vs-custom ratio; CAC trend; **logo + revenue from referrals**; reference count.
- **Benchmark:** Palantir's "scale back to product"; ELG virality (partners invite partners).

## PART 5 — The adoption system (how clients actually stick)

Selling ≠ adopting. Adoption is engineered:

1. **Install the operating discipline, not just the tool.** The GTM manual's core: *every claim should be captured, validated, protected, agreement-linked, eligibility-reviewed, and visible to leadership.* Adoption = that becoming **the weekly habit**. Reven ships the **operating cadence** (weekly claim review, monthly CFO partner-revenue review) as a built-in ritual, not a feature they have to invent.
2. **Activation milestones, instrumented.** Define the "activated" event per tier (SMB: first contribution graph + 10 claims; Enterprise: finance-accepted evidence pack). Drive to it with in-product guidance + CS outreach. Best-in-class activation 65–75%.
3. **Time-to-value targets** as first-class metrics: SMB minutes; Enterprise weeks. Faster TTV → +20–40% retention/upsell.
4. **CS playbooks tied to value milestones,** not check-ins. Pooled CSM for SMB/Growth; dedicated CSM + Solution Architect for enterprise/gov.
5. **Health scoring → expansion triggers:** usage signals (active partners, claims/week, disputes resolved) auto-flag expansion or churn risk.
6. **The settlement carrot (Phase 2):** once they trust the ledger, settlement is the natural deepening — the moment Reven becomes infrastructure they can't remove.

## PART 6 — Pricing & packaging in service of the motion (already designed — here's how it *drives* the flywheel)

From [`Reven_Pricing_Executive_Summary.md`](Reven_Pricing_Executive_Summary.md), read through the GTM lens:

- **Land:** SME **SAR 50/mo self-serve** (published) = the SMB wedge; **"Reven Sprint" pilot** = the enterprise wedge. Both cheap to enter.
- **Price metric = active (transacting) partners, unlimited seats.** Buyer-friendly (no per-seat adoption tax that Salesforce PRM/Magentrix impose), category-standard (Kiflo/Impartner/Crossbeam), and it **expands automatically** as their program grows.
- **Refuse the % take-rate early** (impact.com ~2.5%, PartnerStack take-fee) — the finance buyer's most-resented model; refusing it is the **neutral-Switzerland wedge**.
- **≥3 expansion dimensions** (bands + modules + compliance + entities + settlement) = the NRR engine that $100M+ SaaS converge on, with no bill-shock.
- **Compliance ladder (L0→L3)** = a GCC moat incumbents can't retrofit; charge for it now.
- **Margin guard:** subscription + ~100%-GM data dominate gross profit; settlement/% layer **capped, fenced, reported separately** → valued like software, not a payments company.
- **Public vs custom:** publish SME; "starting-from" SMB; sales-assisted mid-market; custom-quote semi-gov & enterprise (procurement expects it).

## PART 7 — KSA/GCC execution levers (don't lose the local context)

- **VAT 15%, priced in SAR, billed annually local** → +15–25% close rates; **old 5%-VAT assumptions break deals at procurement.**
- **Gov/semi-gov procurement = 6–12 months** → run a parallel long-cycle motion; milestone disbursement (they pay in arrears); Etimad/RHQ-aware.
- **Local trust:** KSA buyers are skeptical of foreign-only vendors → **in-Kingdom operations + local SI/reseller partner** in year one; founder visibility (GITEX, speaking) compounds.
- **In-Kingdom data residency + Arabic/RTL + Sharia structuring** aren't features — they're **deal-gates** for enterprise/gov.
- **No standalone Saudi-built partner/commission SaaS exists** → Reven has no direct local competitor; the category-creation burden is the main cost, not competition.

## PART 8 — The execution sequence (what to do, in order)

**First 90 days (prove the motion):**
1. Build the **SMB self-serve path** end-to-end: signup → connect CRM → contribution graph + claim ledger → activation milestone → self-serve upgrade. Instrument activation + TTFV.
2. Sign **3–5 enterprise design partners** from an *existing budget line*; run the **Reven Sprint → paid pilot**; deliver a **finance-accepted evidence pack** for each. (This validates the ≥SAR 95–110K willingness-to-pay — the #1 thing to test.)
3. Stand up the **Solutions-reports-to-Product** loop so pilot learnings become features from day one.
4. KSA-localize: SAR + 15% VAT + Arabic/RTL + one local SI partner.

**Months 4–9 (Phase 1 scale):** turn pilots into annual contracts + references; open expansion (bands, modules, compliance L1); publish SMB, "starting-from" SMB+; hit first NRR signals.

**Months 10–18 (into Phase 2):** harden; open the **settlement-layer** expansion conversation with trusting accounts (capped per-payout fee); push implementation to SI partners; drive blended NRR toward 120%.

## PART 9 — The dashboard (what to watch) & failure modes

**Watch (by track):**
| Stage | SMB metric | Enterprise metric |
|---|---|---|
| Acquire | signups, CAC | Sprints booked, pipeline |
| Activate | activation % (→65–75%), TTFV (<1 day) | pilot value-proof acceptance, TTV (≤8 wk) |
| Convert | free→paid % | pilot→paid %, ACV vs SAR 95–110K floor |
| Expand | band/module upgrades | **NRR (≥118% ent)**, modules live |
| Compound | referral logos, virality (partner invites) | references, config-vs-custom ratio |

**Failure modes to avoid:**
- **Running enterprise onboarding on SMBs (or vice-versa)** — self-serve users want speed; enterprise wants white-glove. Mismatch kills both.
- **SME tier with a human in the loop** — one support call erases a year's revenue; keep it fully self-serve.
- **Letting Solutions report to Sales** — you become a services firm (bespoke one-offs, no productization). Report to Product.
- **Promising settlement/payouts in Phase 1** — breaks phase discipline and trust; eligibility is a *preview* until Phase 2.
- **Introducing a % take-rate early** — forfeits the neutral-Switzerland wedge the finance buyer values most.
- **Old-VAT / USD-only pricing in KSA** — dies at procurement.
