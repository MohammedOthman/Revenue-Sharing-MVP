## SLIDE · MARKET SIZE

### We're not selling a tool into a small market. We're governing a flow in a huge one.

> The mistake everyone makes is sizing the *partner software* market (~$1–3B globally — small).
> The money that matters is the **partner revenue** that flows through companies every year.
> That pool is ~100× bigger, and it's growing 14–23% a year. We price on the flow, so our
> ceiling rises with it.

**Starting market: Saudi Arabia — then the GCC, then every market with partners.**

### The market in three numbers (Saudi Arabia, per year)

| | What it is | Size | Why it's real |
|---|---|---|---|
| **THE PRIZE** | Partner-attributed revenue flowing through KSA tech companies — the money Reven governs | **$15–20B/yr** | ~$20–25B of enterprise tech revenue × ~70% that moves through a partner. Grows 14–23%/yr. |
| **WHAT THEY'LL PAY** *(SAM)* | Annual software spend Reven can capture | **~$200M** ($150–400M) | ~2,000–4,500 Saudi companies run real partner programs × $50–90k each |
| **WHAT WE TAKE FIRST** *(3-yr SOM)* | Realistic ARR by Year 3 | **$5–10M ARR** | 60–120 logos, compliance-led beachhead |

### The hook a CFO can't ignore

**$40–480M / year is leaking** in Saudi tech alone — wrong attribution, duplicate payouts, and
the people paid to chase it manually — on top of **$1.2–1.6B** of partner payouts that move every
year. **We price Reven below the leakage we recover — so the product pays for itself.**

### Why the number only gets bigger

- **The whole-economy ceiling is $40–70B** once we move past tech into insurance, banking,
  logistics, e-commerce — every sector where commission *is* the business model.
- **Settlement doubles it again.** Once we move the money (not just record it), we earn on a
  **$1.2–1.6B+ payout base** plus a **$38.5B cross-border corridor** — roughly doubling revenue
  per customer.
- **GCC replication multiplies the whole thing.** Same compliance patterns, UAE e-invoicing
  already live.

## HOW WE SCALE — END TO END (bottom band, or a companion "Scale" slide)

### One customer grows three ways. One market becomes many.

**We grow ACV inside an account, then repeat the motion across segments, then across countries —
each step is a higher price *and* a deeper moat.**

#### 1 · Grow *inside* the account — Land → Expand → Own

| Stage | What we sell | ACV | Net revenue retention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LAND** · Capture | The claim ledger — stop the leakage | ~$40–80k | — |
| **EXPAND** · Settle | Finance-grade evidence + payouts (CFO buys in) | $25–250k | 115–120% |
| **OWN** · Orchestrate | Partner P&L, the quarterly invest/cut decision, the partner network | 6–7 figures | 120%+ |

> Each layer earns the right to the next. Clean records make attribution worth it; attribution
> makes finance trust us; finance trust lets us move money; moving money gives us the data to run
> the decisions. That's why customers expand instead of churn.

#### 2 · Repeat the motion *across segments* (Saudi)

**IT channel & distribution + RHQs** *(beachhead — densest partner flow, sharpest compliance pain)*
→ **Fintech** → **Insurance** *(commission + clawback is literally the business)*
→ **E-commerce 3P / affiliates** *(huge payout volume)* → **Family conglomerates** *(one deal, many entities)*.

#### 3 · Expand *across countries* — starting where no global tool can follow

**Saudi first, on purpose.** Our wedge is compliance global incumbents can't retrofit:
**ZATCA e-invoicing, withholding tax, PDPL, and Sharia-compliant revenue share.** That's
non-discretionary budget that doesn't get cut — and it's defensible. **Then GCC** (UAE e-invoicing
already live, the patterns repeat), then the wider region, then any market where companies grow
through partners.

**Why now makes the timing exact:** ZATCA Wave 24 pulls Saudi's ~600k VAT-registered companies into
integration-grade e-invoicing by June 2026; ~600 regional HQs are compelled in; **14,303 new
foreign-investment licenses landed in 2024 alone** — greenfield buyers with no legacy tool to rip
out. And AWS, Azure, Google and Oracle all open Saudi regions in 2026 — a wave of co-sell deals
that all need attribution, arriving exactly as we do.

## Speaker-notes rationale (plain English)

- **Why a "flow" not a "category."** Most decks size the PRM software market and land on a tiny TAM.
  We size the *revenue under management* — backed by ~70% of enterprise IT going through a partner
  (Canalys) and ~95% of Microsoft's revenue being partner-influenced.
- **Why we lead with leakage, not TAM.** TAM convinces a VC; leakage convinces a CFO. The $40–480M
  number is a hard-dollar figure the buyer can underwrite ("we recover more than we cost").
- **Why the SOM looks modest — and why that's a feature.** $5–10M ARR in 3 years signals discipline,
  not a hockey stick. Upside lives in the three expansion axes + settlement.
- **Why Saudi-first is a strength.** The compliance wedge is the moat. Start in the hardest-to-copy
  market, then replicate the pattern across the GCC.
- **Methodology footnote (small, bottom of slide):** *"Figures are analyst estimates triangulated
  top-down (macro → tech → partner-routed revenue) and bottom-up (companies × program incidence ×
  ACV), cross-checked against KSA enterprise-software spend and comparable-vendor scale. FX:
  $1 ≈ SAR 3.75. Sources: GASTAT, SAMA, CST, IDC, Canalys, ZATCA, MISA."*
