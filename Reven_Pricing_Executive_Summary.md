# Reven — Pricing & Tiers: Executive Summary

**Reven** is the enterprise, finance-grade **control layer for partner revenue** — the system of record for the partner-revenue claim, attribution, eligibility, statements, and settlement. This is the recommended pricing architecture and tier structure. All figures are **benchmarked recommendations to validate** (not list-locked), shown in **USD and SAR** (peg 3.75; KSA contracts in SAR, VAT 15%).

---

## 1. The recommendation, in one line

> Sell a **hybrid platform subscription priced on *active (transacting) partners*, with unlimited internal seats**, packaged Good-Better-Best by capability depth, sold annually above a **~$25–30K high-touch floor** — with a **stackable GCC compliance/residency premium**, a **fenced fixed-fee implementation**, and a **flat per-payout fee** added only once Reven runs settlement. **Refuse the resented % take-rate early**; introduce a capped, Sharia-structured fee on revenue-under-management only at scale.

**Three metrics, kept deliberately separate:**

| | Definition | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Value metric** | Trusted partner-attributed revenue realized | What the customer actually buys; the North Star |
| **Pricing metric** | Active (transacting) partners, banded × tier | Buyer-friendly, category-standard, expands with the program — evolving toward *attributed-revenue* as programs mature |
| **Expansion metric** | partners → modules → compliance tier → entities/countries → settlement volume → data licensing | The NRR engine, assembled incrementally |

**Unlimited internal seats at every tier.** The active-partner *band* — not seats and not claims — is the headline scaling unit. Minimum ACVs gate entry (no sub-$6K Reven); any deal needing a sales + implementation motion must clear **~$25–30K**.

## 3. What's in each package

Four outcome-based packages map to the tiers — fencing on **scale, compliance, deployment, depth, and support**, never on product *integrity* (attribution-of-record, eligibility, and the audit trail are in every tier):

- **Partner Operations (SME)** — replace spreadsheets: partner registry, programs, claim ledger, attribution-of-record, protection windows, evidence, duplicate detection.
- **Partner Revenue Control (SMB)** — add finance control: statements, disputes, finance evidence pack, eligibility, conflict management, revenue-event matching, reversals.
- **Partner Revenue Orchestration (Mid-market)** — add complexity: multi-party attribution, clawback-by-netting, multi-entity (lite), program/ecosystem analytics, advanced workflows, API.
- **Ecosystem Enterprise (Semi-gov + Large enterprise)** — add scale & sovereignty: SSO/SCIM, in-Kingdom data residency, dedicated tenant, sandbox, premium SLA, dedicated CSM + Solution Architect.

## 4. Commercial model

- **Implementation** — priced separately (fixed-fee + milestone), **fenced below 20% of revenue**, pushed to local SI partners over time. Templated/waivable for SME; **15–30% of ACV** at mid-market; **0.5–1.0×+ ACV** for enterprise/semi-gov (config, data migration, integrations, Arabic, accreditation, change management).
- **Compliance ladder — the GCC wedge incumbents can't retrofit:** L0 Standard → **L1** ZATCA/WHT/PDPL → **L2** in-Kingdom residency / dedicated tenancy (**+15–30%**) → **L3** Sharia-certified (Ju'ala/Wakala + fatwa). Charge now while it differentiates; expect to rebundle L1 toward table-stakes by 2026.
- **Add-ons (expansion + margin protection):** partner-band blocks, additional entities/countries, programs, integrations/connectors, advanced analytics, premium API, AI-assisted attribution (metered/capped), premium support/SLA, managed operations. Recurring add-ons are high-margin software; **services and AI are always fenced/capped**.
- **Contracting:** annual default; multi-year for enterprise/gov with a **fixed-% escalation floor** and auto-renew. **Annual prepay** for private deals (beats the GCC cash cycle); **milestone disbursement** for semi-gov (which pays in arrears, not upfront).
- **Discount guardrails:** governed on **pocket price** (net of channel/SI margin of ~15–30%), not list. **≤25% default** (≤15% SMB; up to ~30% strategic with give-gets — term, volume, reference, expansion rights). Hard floor: **≥70% blended gross margin**. Approval ladder by discount depth.

**Public vs custom:** publish **SME**; **"starting from"** for SMB; **sales-assisted** mid-market; **custom-quote** semi-government and large enterprise (procurement expects it).

---

## 5. Why this structure

1. **It matches how the buyer budgets** — partner count is the category norm (Kiflo, Impartner, Crossbeam); unlimited seats removes the adoption tax that per-seat models (Salesforce PRM, Magentrix) impose.
2. **It refuses the most-resented model** — the % take-rate (impact.com ~2.5%, PartnerStack take-fee) that the finance buyer explicitly rejects; refusing it early *is* the neutral-Switzerland wedge.
3. **It expands automatically** — bands + modules + compliance + entities give the ≥3 pricing dimensions that $100M+ SaaS converge on, without bill-shock.
4. **It fits GCC procurement** — committed annual/multi-year SAR licenses with line-item add-ons, not usage bill-shock.
5. **It protects the margin and the multiple** — subscription + ~100%-GM data dominate gross profit; the settlement/% layer is capped, fenced, and reported separately, so Reven is valued like software.

## 6. What must be validated before prices are locked

| Assumption | Test | If wrong |
|---|---|---|
| Buyers pay **≥$25–30K** for finance-grade attribution over cheaper PRM | 3–5 paid pilots from an *existing* budget line | Reven is an $6–18K SMB tool — re-baseline ACV and motion |
| **"Active = transacted"** doesn't undercharge high-value-per-partner accounts | Instrument partner-count vs attributed-revenue across pilots | Shift the headline metric to attributed-revenue bands sooner |
| Compliance/residency premium **and** semi-gov deployment leave **≥70% GM** | Loaded cost-to-serve model (KSA rails, ZATCA, KYB, residency, support) | Raise the premium/license, or decline sub-scale gov deals |

> **Bottom line:** lead with a predictable per-active-partner subscription the Head of Partnerships can say yes to; deepen into finance control, settlement, and sovereignty as the CFO becomes the buyer; capture **10–20% of measurable customer value** (≈ sub-30 bps of partner-attributed revenue) through the subscription and modules — never a visible percentage of the partner's money.
