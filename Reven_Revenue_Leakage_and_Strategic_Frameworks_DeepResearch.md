## PART 1 — THE LEAKAGE MAP (quantified)

### 1.1 Verification verdict — real vs. myth

| Claim | Verdict | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| "EY: companies lose 1–5% of revenue/EBITDA to leakage" | ❌ **MYTH** — untraceable to any EY publication; snowballed from a 2021 Medium post citing "Forbes and EY" with no link | **MGI Research:** SaaS lose **1–5% of EBITDA / 3–5% of ARR** (real, traceable) |
| "42% of companies suffer revenue leakage" | ⚠️ Untraceable; attributed inconsistently to EY *and* MGI | Don't cite as fact |
| "8% / 7% commission error rate" | ⚠️ Vendor marketing, no primary | Use **3%** (Xactly) |
| "30–60% of MDF goes unclaimed" | ⚠️ Industry truism, no Gartner/Forrester study behind it | Cite as "widely-cited industry estimate (vendor-sourced)" |
| Gartner "3–5% comp overpayment" | 🟡 Number well-corroborated; exact report ID not locked | "widely cited, ~3–5%" |

### 1.2 Load-bearing benchmarks (HIGH confidence)

| # | Mechanism | Benchmark | Source (year) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sales-comp inaccuracy** | **83% of companies** have inaccurate payouts; **~3% avg error** → **$15M leaked on a $500M pool**; ~10% of revenue spent on commissions | Xactly (2018) |
| 2 | **Spreadsheet fragility** | **~88% of spreadsheets contain errors**; ~5% cell-error rate; ~75% of enterprises run incentives on spreadsheets | Panko/EuSpRIG; IBM via Everstage |
| 3 | **Trade-promotion ineffectiveness** | **59% of trade promotions lose money globally / 72% in the US**; CPG spends **~20% of revenue** on it | Nielsen via McKinsey |
| 4 | **Double-crediting / over-attribution** | **16% of orgs pay on 130%+ of actual revenue**; **58% credit ≥2 sellers** per deal | Alexander Group / WorldatWork |
| 5 | **Affiliate fraud** | **~9% baseline** (conservative); fake traffic **10%→17%** (2020→2022); **>$3.4B lost (2022)** | CHEQ |
| 6 | **Retail deductions / chargebacks** | **5–7% of revenue** (SPS Commerce); **5–15% of gross sales** (Inmar) | SPS Commerce; Inmar |
| 7 | **Ad-tech "unknown delta"** | Only **51% of ad spend reaches publishers**; **15% of total spend unattributable to ANY party**. 2nd study cut it to 3% **only with manual reconciliation** | ISBA/PwC (2020, 2022) |
| 8 | **Music "black box"** | **~$2.5B global** unmatched; **$424.4M** US mechanicals (2007–2020) to The MLC; **>$700M held unmatched** by mid-2023 | Billboard; The MLC; Digital Music News |
| 9 | **Telecom revenue leakage** | **13.6% (2007)** → **0.52% today** once an assurance discipline existed | Analysys/Subex (2007); TM Forum (2024) |
| 10 | **Telecom inter-carrier fraud** | **$38.95B ≈ 2.5% of revenue** (2023) | CFCA |
| 11 | **Rev-rec error → restatement** | Revenue recognition = **~38% of all restatements** (1997–2002) and **~40% of fraud cases** | GAO-03-138; CPA Journal |
| 12 | **B2B rebate leakage** | **2–4% of potential rebate revenue** lost/unclaimed annually | Enable |

**The master pattern:** across every model, the take-rate is rarely the problem — **the leak is that no
neutral party owns a shared system of record** for money two companies make together. The worst-leakage
domains (programmatic ad tech, music royalties, multi-tier channel, BaaS) have the *most intermediaries
and least shared truth*. Telecom proves the converse: leakage fell ~26× once a reconciliation discipline
existed.

**Adjacent disciplines worth importing wholesale:** Telecom **Revenue Assurance** (mature taxonomy +
~$6–7B tooling market), retail **deduction-recovery** (contingency-fee model), and music's **MLC
matching engine** are the same problem Reven solves in other industries — proof the category is real and
a source of proven playbooks.

## The three sentences that summarize it

1. **Leakage is universal and quantified** — 3% of commissions (Xactly), 59–72% of trade promos
   (Nielsen), 130%-of-revenue payouts (Alexander Group), 15% of ad spend unattributable (ISBA/PwC),
   $700M+ of music royalties unmatched (MLC) — and telecom proved it collapses ~26× once someone owns
   the reconciliation.
2. **The cause is always the same** — no neutral party owns a shared system of record across the parties
   splitting the money.
3. **Reven's defensible play** — be the **System of Record → System of Intelligence**, monetized as a
   **low-rake toll-booth on the flow**, defended by **counter-positioning + compliance/neutrality +
   switching costs**, entered through a **compliance beachhead**, compounded into a **clearing-house +
   network** — exactly the gap every revenue-sharing model leaves open.
