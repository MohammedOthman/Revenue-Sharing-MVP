## Part 1 — The core narrative (Raskin's 5 acts)

**Act 1 — The shift.** For a century, a company's revenue was its own. That world is gone. Today
revenue is created *between* companies — resellers, marketplaces, platforms, banks, agencies, co-sell.
The fastest-growing companies don't grow alone. **Growth itself has become a shared act.**

**Act 2 — Winners and losers.** When revenue is shared, the truth about it is shared too — and shared
truth doesn't exist yet. The moment a partner helps close a deal, the simplest question — *what do they
get paid?* — has a different answer in every system. Two kinds of companies are emerging: the ones who
can **prove, settle, and trust** the revenue they make with partners, and the ones who can't. The first
turn partnerships into their biggest growth engine. **The second quietly retreat from the very channel
that was supposed to scale them.**

**Act 3 — The villain.** The enemy was never a competitor. It's the **spreadsheet truce** — the fragile,
manual, "good-enough" agreement that holds while partner revenue is small and **breaks the moment it
matters.** And it's a tax you can measure:
- **3% of commissions** paid in error; **83% of companies** have inaccurate payouts (Xactly)
- Programs pay on **130% of actual revenue** through double-crediting (Alexander Group)
- **59–72% of trade promotions lose money** (Nielsen via McKinsey)
- In ad-tech, **15% of every dollar is unattributable** (ISBA/PwC); in music, **$700M+ sits unmatched** (The MLC)

The pattern is always the same: **no neutral party owns the record of money two companies make together.**

**Act 4 — The promised land.** Imagine partner revenue you trust like your own. Every contribution
captured. Every claim checked against the agreement, the deal, the payment. Every payout **proven to
finance before it moves.** Every partner able to see exactly where they stand. Partner revenue stops
being the liability your CFO fears and becomes **the growth engine your CEO can scale on purpose.**

**Act 5 — The proof.** This already happened once. **Telecom** had this exact problem and built a
discipline to fix it: leakage fell from **13.6% (2007) to 0.52% (2024)** once someone owned the
reconciliation. Reven brings that discipline to every company that grows through partners — starting
where the pain is sharpest and the moat is deepest: **compliance-native markets (ZATCA, withholding tax,
Sharia) that global incumbents can't retrofit.**

## Part 2 — The commercial insight (the reframe)

> **You think your partner problem is a *relationship* problem. It's a *revenue* problem.**
>
> Conventional wisdom: nurture partners, give them a portal, track their activity. But every serious
> partnership raises the same questions — *who contributed, who deserves credit, who gets paid, which
> partner is profitable?* — and you answer them by hand, **paying a tax you can't see** to a system of
> record that doesn't exist.
>
> The companies that win the next decade won't be the ones with the **most partners.** They'll be the
> ones who can **prove which partners create value, deserve credit, should be paid, and are worth
> scaling.**

## Part 3 — Positioning & the Why

**Positioning:** For **B2B companies whose growth runs through partners**, who **can't prove, settle, or
trust the revenue they create together**, **Reven** is **the Partner Revenue Operating System** that
**turns every partner contribution into attributed, proven, compliant, paid revenue.** Unlike **CRM,
ERP, PRM, or payout tools that each own one slice**, Reven **owns the whole journey — claim to
settlement — as the neutral system of record between the companies that share the money.**

**Why / How / What:**
- **Why:** We believe the future of revenue is shared — and **shared revenue deserves to be trusted.**
- **How:** By making **the partner revenue claim** the single, neutral, provable record between two companies.
- **What:** The Partner Revenue Operating System.

## Part 4 — The manifesto (category POV / cold open)

> Companies don't grow alone anymore.
> They grow through partners — and the money they make together is growing with them.
> But that money lives in spreadsheets, email threads, and memory. Five systems, five answers, zero agreement.
> So the money leaks. The partners stop trusting. Finance can't sign off. And the channel that was supposed to scale you starts to scare you.
> It was never a relationship problem. **It was always a revenue problem.**
> Every dollar created with a partner deserves to be **claimed, proven, and paid** — with the same trust as the revenue you make alone.
> That's why we're building the **operating system for partner revenue.**
> Because the future of revenue is shared. **And shared revenue deserves to be trusted.**
