## Part B — The six narratives

### Narrative 1 — THE TRUST UTILITY (compliance wedge)
**Spine:** *In the Gulf, a partner payout legally can't move without ZATCA/WHT/Sharia-correct proof — so
Reven becomes the compliance-native record finance is required to trust, and global tools can't retrofit.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* compliance is a non-discretionary budget floor, not a feature. *Guiding policy:* own the proof layer via **Counter-Positioning + Cornered Resource** (licenses, in-Kingdom residency, Sharia fatwa). *Where/how:* KSA-regulated B2B with cross-border payouts (IT-channel × RHQ); win on compliance depth incumbents won't build.
- **Tactical:** ZATCA-cleared-XML ingestion (free attribution exhaust); WHT engine + RHQ 0%-WHT check; Ju'ala/Wakala revenue-share templates; **tower-and-moat** gameplay; finance-grade evidence pack.
- **Operational:** *WIG:* "0 → 5 finance-accepted evidence packs by M9." *Lead measure:* ZATCA-clean packs reviewed by a real finance reviewer/week. *KR:* time-to-first-claim <14 days; 3–5 design partners. *Cadence:* weekly catchball with design-partner finance teams.
- **Key risk:** ZATCA Wave 24 makes L1 compliance table-stakes by Jun 2026 → edge commoditizes. *Mitigation:* bank the wedge now; climb to settlement/intelligence before it erodes.

### Narrative 2 — THE CATEGORY KING ("Partner Revenue OS")
**Spine:** *No one owns partner revenue end-to-end; Reven names and rules a new category and takes the
~76% of economics the category king captures.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* the journey falls through the gaps between CRM/ERP/PRM/payments. *Guiding policy:* **category design** — POV ("revenue problem, not relationship problem"); **branding power** over time. *Where/how:* the white space; win by defining the language.
- **Tactical:** publish the manifesto + a **leakage benchmark report**; a **lightning-strike** launch; design-partner logos; ride the **Gartner PRM→PERM** shift with analysts.
- **Operational:** *WIG:* own "Partner Revenue OS" in N analyst notes/press by M12. *Lead measure:* inbound using the category language. *KR:* share-of-voice; content/demo cadence that makes "5 systems, 5 answers" visceral.
- **Key risk:** category creation is slow/expensive and you're pre-MVP. *Mitigation:* let the compliance wedge fund it; don't lead early sales with "category."

### Narrative 3 — THE TOLL-BOOTH / SETTLEMENT NETWORK
**Spine:** *Once Reven owns the claim and the proof, it settles the money — a low, capped fee on a
$1.2–1.6B+ payout flow that compounds with every customer's ecosystem.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* value uncaps only when you sit on the flow, not the record. *Guiding policy:* **toll-booth / tax-on-a-flow** + **clearing-house netting**, governed by **"A Rake Too Far"** (price below leakage recovered). *7 Powers:* establish **Switching Costs + Network Economies** (Takeoff window).
- **Tactical:** per-payout fixed fee + capped bps (not % of gross); A2A rails (Lean open banking); cross-border WHT+FX module; multilateral **netting/novation** engine; cloud-marketplace **co-sell (CPPO) reconciliation**.
- **Operational:** *WIG:* "0% → X% of customer payout volume settled in-product." *Lead measure:* $ settled/week. *KR:* NRR >120%; reconciliation SLA; dispute-resolution time.
- **Key risk:** settlement adds regulatory/operational weight + take-rate resistance. *Mitigation:* gate on a trusted ledger first; fixed-fee shape; heed the pricing red-team's bps caution.

### Narrative 4 — THE ANTI-LEAKAGE CFO PLAY
**Spine:** *Companies leak 3–15% of every partner dollar to a record that doesn't exist; Reven recovers
more than it costs — a self-funding purchase the CFO signs.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* leakage is real, measured, CFO-owned (Xactly 3%; Alexander Group 130%; Nielsen 59–72%). *Guiding policy:* **leakage-funded ROI**; import the **telecom revenue-assurance** discipline (13.6%→0.52%). *Where/how:* finance buyer in partner-heavy orgs.
- **Tactical:** a "find-the-leak" assessment; **recovery-share / contingency pricing**; a leakage dashboard (their own "unknown delta"); ASC 606 evidence; **clawback automation**.
- **Operational:** *WIG:* "Identify $X of recoverable leakage across 5 assessments." *Lead measure:* $ leakage surfaced per assessment. *KR:* leakage-rate reduced / $ recovered; monthly CFO-review cadence.
- **Key risk:** ROI proof needs real data you don't have pre-MVP; data-hygiene gating. *Mitigation:* ZATCA-XML + CSV-first onboarding; lead with the assessment to manufacture the proof.

### Narrative 5 — NEUTRAL SWITZERLAND (counter-positioning vs AppDirect)
**Spine:** *Every settlement player is structurally conflicted — marketplaces take a cut, banks push
their rails. Reven is the only neutral, two-sided record both companies trust, the one thing AppDirect
can't be.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* AppDirect (Tackle + PartnerStack) could ship bilateral settlement in 2–4 quarters — but only as the conflicted marketplace. *Guiding policy:* **Counter-Positioning** — neutrality is a model the incumbent won't copy without cannibalizing its cut. *7 Powers:* Origination-stage power to lock now.
- **Tactical:** **dual-entry bilateral truth** (one record, two ASC-606 views); **Schelling-point** positioning ("the neutral default"); a public no-take-rate-on-gross pledge; data-co-op integrations (Crossbeam/Reveal).
- **Operational:** *WIG:* "N relationships where *both* companies transact on Reven." *Lead measure:* second-side activations/month. *KR:* live two-sided links; immutable audit-trail coverage.
- **Key risk:** two-sided cold-start (chicken-and-egg) + AppDirect's distribution and clock. *Mitigation:* start single-sided (be one side's system of record), light the second side later; move on the window now.

### Narrative 6 — THE INTELLIGENCE / DATA-NETWORK ENDGAME
**Spine:** *Structured partner-revenue data, owned across tenants, lets Reven move from recording money
to deciding it — the system of intelligence that runs the quarterly invest/cut decision.*

- **Strategic:** *Diagnosis:* scattered data can't teach; structured in one place it can. *Guiding policy:* **System of Record → System of Intelligence** (Jerry Chen) + an *honest* data-network effect (paired with switching costs, per a16z) → **increasing-returns flywheel**; own the capital-allocation decision. *7 Powers:* Stability-stage Process Power.
- **Tactical:** cross-tenant attribution/dedup graph; dispute-prediction & risk-scoring; partner P&L + incentive simulation; anonymized **benchmark/data licensing**.
- **Operational:** *WIG:* "% of partner decisions (terms changed, scaled/cut) made in-product." *Lead measure:* decisions taken in QBR using Reven. *KR:* benchmark coverage, model accuracy.
- **Key risk:** "data moat" is easy to overclaim and needs scale first (a16z). *Mitigation:* don't sell it early; earn it via record+settlement scale; pair with domain depth.

## Part C — Coherence: it's ONE story (and a sequence)

Collapsed into a single Rumelt kernel:

- **Diagnosis:** Revenue is now created *between* companies, but no neutral party owns the shared record — so it leaks, disputes, and can't be trusted or scaled.
- **Guiding policy:** Own the **neutral, compliance-native record** of partner revenue, then **climb the layers**: record → proof → settlement → intelligence.
- **Coherent action (the sequence):**

| Phase | Lead narrative | 7 Powers window |
|---|---|---|
| **LAND (now, pre-seed → MVP)** | **N1 Trust Utility + N4 Anti-Leakage**, framed by **N2 Category** | *Origination:* Counter-Positioning (N5) + Cornered Resource (N1) |
| **EXPAND (V1→V2)** | **N5 Neutral Switzerland** + **N3 Toll-Booth** | *Takeoff:* Switching Costs + Network Economies |
| **OWN (V3)** | **N6 Intelligence Network** | *Stability:* Process Power + Branding (N2) |

**Single-page X-matrix (Hoshin):**
- **Breakthrough objective (True North):** Become the trusted system of record for partner revenue, starting in the GCC.
- **Annual objectives:** (a) 3–5 finance-accepted design partners; (b) own the "Partner Revenue OS" category language; (c) prove the compliance wedge end-to-end.
- **Initiatives:** compliance/evidence engine · leakage assessment · manifesto + benchmark report.
- **Metrics:** time-to-first-claim <14d · 100+ real claims · 70% activation · $ leakage surfaced.

**For the deck now:** lead with the fused **N1+N4** (compliance-wedge, anti-leakage trust utility) as the
LAND, frame it with **N2** (category), answer "what about AppDirect?" with **N5** (neutrality), and sell
the venture upside with **N3 + N6** (toll-booth → intelligence network).
