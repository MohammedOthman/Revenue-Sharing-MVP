## PART 0 — EXECUTIVE SYNTHESIS: what the deep-dives change

**What got confirmed (high confidence):**

Everyone stops at unilateral payouts, money-free account-mapping, or intermediary/single-enterprise settlement.
2. **The GCC compliance wedge is buildable and genuinely hard for incumbents to copy** — ZATCA clearance, the WHT matrix, Sharia-compliant structures, and PDPL data residency are concrete, regulation-anchored differentiators.
3. **The PDR's two hardest architectural bets are externally validated** — deterministic-first identity resolution, and "human-authoritative, model-advisory" attribution (the latter is now also the *EU-AI-Act-compliant* pattern).

**What changed or sharpened (the five calls):**
1. **Lead the pitch with payout accuracy + reconciliation + compliance — not "attribution leakage."** The leakage cost-numbers don't survive verification (the "EY 1–5% of EBITDA" figure is untraceable; a "42% of CFOs" stat appears fabricated; "Partner Attribution Leak" is confirmed unquantified). The hard, non-discretionary demand drivers are **regulatory (ZATCA) and audit/rev-rec (ASC 606/SOX)**.
2. **Don't try to own "partnership orchestration" — own "Partner Revenue / settlement."** "Orchestration" is a vendor/analyst descriptor, already crowded and adoption-fatigued (Forrester-via-secondary: 61% say their ecosystem is "too complex to orchestrate"). The named, adoptable category is **PERM** (Gartner's evolution of PRM, and only a *Market Guide*, signaling an immature market). The ownable, un-occupied slice is the **money/settlement layer**, positioned *adjacent* to Ecosystem-Led Growth.
3. **There is a competitive clock: AppDirect.** AppDirect acquired **Tackle.io (Dec 2025)** and **PartnerStack (April 2026, ~$150–250M reported)** and already runs marketplace-mediated revenue-share + reconciliation + multi-party payouts. It is the single most credible entrant into the white space and could productize bilateral co-sell settlement in **2–4 quarters**. The defensible edge it *structurally cannot* claim is **neutrality** (it's the marketplace taking a cut). Move while that window is open.
4. **Make cross-tenant identity the wedge, not a feature, and monetize at settlement.** The one proven B2B network-ignition mechanic (Crossbeam) is privacy-preserving cross-tenant overlap + freemium cold-start. ## PART 1 — THE MOAT, VALIDATED (competitive teardown + settlement landscape)

Two independent tracks (a vendor-by-vendor teardown and a settlement/reconciliation-landscape scan) converged on the same verdict.

### 1.1 Verdict: the bilateral settlement system-of-record is genuinely unowned at the core (~75% confidence)

Every player stops short in one of three ways:
- **Unilateral payout/commission** (PartnerStack, Impartner, ZINFI, Kiflo, Introw, Zomentum): one side funds, the other receives; no shared reconcile/clawback/dispute ledger both parties audit.
- **Money-free account-mapping / intelligence** (Crossbeam, PartnerTap, Superglue, Allbound→Channelscaler, Unifyr): explicitly do not touch money.
- **Intermediary- or single-enterprise settlement** (AppDirect = settles *as the marketplace*; RecVue, SETTL = run inside *one* company's stack; telecom interconnect = each carrier runs its *own* instance and reconciles by exchange + negotiation).

**None is a neutral, two-sided, dispute-and-audit-clean ledger jointly trusted by two independent co-selling companies.** The analysts on both tracks actively hunted for a disconfirming product and did not find one.

### 1.2 The competitive clock — AppDirect (the headline new finding)

- **AppDirect acquired Tackle.io (Dec 2025) and PartnerStack (April 2026, ~$150–250M reported).** It now holds marketplace + PRM + the largest partner-payout network + reconciliation rails — "get found, get chosen, get paid." *(VERIFIED across BusinessWire, BetaKit, TheNextWeb, ERP.today; the deal value is "source familiar," CONTESTED.)*
- **Why it's the #1 threat:** it has the capability; the only gap is *positioning + neutrality*. It could ship bilateral co-sell settlement in 2–4 quarters.
- **Why you can still win:** AppDirect is the marketplace taking a cut — it is **structurally non-neutral**. A startup's ownable position is the **neutral Switzerland both sides trust**, which a marketplace conflicts out of.
- **Others to watch:** **WorkSpan** ("Partner Revenue Flows," Series D 2025) — ~12 months out if it commits, but co-sell-pipeline DNA, not finance-grade. **SETTL** (native-Salesforce Partner Revenue Operations) and **RecVue** (RevOS) are the closest *finance-grade* pure-plays but are *single-sided* today (~12–18 months from a neutral two-sided pivot).

### 1.3 Why it's unowned — because it's *hard*, not unwanted

Demand is well-evidenced (a $4M Capital One affiliate-commission-diversion suit; telecom dispute-by-negotiation; ~25% of RevOps time on manual reconciliation). The barriers are the moat:
1. **Money movement → licensing.** Touching funds makes you a PayFac/money-transmitter (FBO accounts, state MTLs). *Mitigation: be the ledger-of-record WITHOUT moving money first; add settlement later.*
2. **ASC 606 principal-vs-agent.** The two companies must often book the *same* shared revenue differently (one gross/principal, one net/agent). A shared ledger must emit **two each-correct representations** — non-trivial, and a core reason finance teams keep separate books.
3. **Neutrality / data-sensitivity trust.** Both parties must cede authority to a neutral system holding competitively sensitive revenue data (the friction Crossbeam solves only via escrow — and it still doesn't settle money).
4. **Heterogeneity.** Splits, tiers, clawbacks, disputes are bespoke per contract (which is why the working examples are *vertical* — telecom, healthcare VBC, publishing royalties). Today's examples are vertical only because no one has yet generalized the rule engine; Reven's horizontal bet is to make that per-contract heterogeneity **configurable across industries**, not rebuilt per vertical.

**The sharpened wedge:** a **neutral, dual-sided ledger-of-record** that emits each side its own **ASC-606-correct, audit-ready statement that provably ties out**, with a built-in dispute/clawback workflow — *without moving money initially* (dodge the MTL trap). Add basis-points-on-flow once the ledger is trusted.

## PART 2 — THE GCC WEDGE, DE-RISKED (ZATCA + WHT + Sharia)

### 2.1 ZATCA Phase-2 cleared e-invoicing — the builder's pipeline

For a B2B (Standard) invoice, the lifecycle is concrete and codeable:
1. Assemble **UBL 2.1 XML** (invoice type 388, subtype 01; seller/buyer VAT IDs, line items, VAT).
2. Generate the chaining values: **UUID**, **ICV** (sequential counter), **PIH** (base64 SHA-256 of the previous invoice — chains off the previous *submitted* doc even if rejected).
3. Hash + **XAdES-BES** sign (ECDSA over **secp256k1**, SHA-256) using the **CSID** certificate.
4. Build the **TLV QR** (tags 1–9).
5. **POST to the clearance API** (real-time). ZATCA validates schema + ~200 business rules + signature + chain, then **applies its own stamp + QR** and returns the **CLEARED** invoice.
6. **Only after CLEARED** may you send it to the buyer. Persist the cleared XML; its hash becomes the next PIH.

*(B2C/Simplified is different: your system stamps it and you merely **report** within 24h.)*

- **Onboarding (per tenant):** CSR → OTP (Fatoora portal) → Compliance CSID → compliance checks → **Production CSID (~1yr, renewable).** Production host `gw-fatoora.zatca.gov.sa` (the old `gw-apic-gov.gazt.gov.sa` was decommissioned Q1 2025).
- **No mandatory software certification** — ZATCA publishes an *indicative* provider list; **legal liability rests on the taxpayer**, not the software vendor.
- **Multi-tenant burden:** each customer VAT registration needs its **own CSID + private key** — you cannot share one across tenants. **Per-tenant key custody/rotation/renewal is the dominant security obligation.**

**Build-vs-buy: buy the clearance/signing layer for v1** (Edicom/Pagero/ClearTax/Wafeq), keep your invoice data model vendor-neutral, and swap to direct ZATCA integration at volume. Direct is feasible (working open-source libs prove it) but the per-tenant crypto identity is high-blast-radius plumbing a product team shouldn't own on day one.

**The 3 hardest parts:** (1) per-tenant cryptographic identity at scale; (2) byte-exact hash-chain + signing (rejected-invoice/concurrency edge cases silently break a tenant's whole chain); (3) **the tax-structuring question, not the tech** — whether you're a "deemed supplier," agent, or payment processor (rules expand **1 Jan 2026**) determines *who issues which cleared invoice to whom*. Settle this with a KSA VAT advisor *before* building.

Revenue-share document mapping: commission = standard invoice (388/01, 15% VAT); **clawback = credit note (381); upward adjustment = debit note (383)** — both go through the same clearance flow and reference the original.

### 2.2 Withholding tax on cross-border revenue-share

- **Rate matrix (High confidence):** royalties **15%**, technical/consulting services **5%**, management fees **20%**; dividends/interest/rent/insurance/freight commonly **5%**; a **15% "other payments" residual** catches most else.
- **A cross-border partner commission/revenue-share most likely lands in the 15% "other payments" bucket** unless it genuinely qualifies as a 5% technical service — *substance-dependent; needs a tax-advisor sign-off or advance ZATCA position.*
- **The Jan-2024 software-payments guideline** sorts payments into royalty (15%) vs. commercial-profits (0%, absent a PE). A revenue-share **tied to distributing/sublicensing software leans royalty (15%)**; pure use/resale can be commercial profits (0%). **Mixed contracts are the grey zone — a 15-point swing on wording.**
- **Mechanics:** the **KSA-resident payer withholds and remits monthly (within 10 days)**; annual return within 120 days; issues a WHT certificate to the partner; treaty relief via Form Q7B + a Tax Residency Certificate. **No US–KSA tax treaty** (US partners face full domestic rates).
- **VAT:** 15%, with **B2B reverse-charge** (recipient self-accounts; broadly cash-neutral if fully taxable). Separate regime from WHT — both can apply to the same payment.

### 2.3 Sharia-compliant revenue-share structures

- **Best fit — Ju'ala (fee-for-result, AAOIFI SS-15):** maps almost exactly to commission-on-sales; uniquely *tolerates* uncertainty about effort/duration. The reward must be clearly defined; earned on result.
- **Strong fit — Wakala (agency-for-fee, AAOIFI SS-46):** for a managed partner program (flat fee + performance incentive). **Hard rule: the agent must never guarantee a minimum return** — that flips it to *riba*.
- **Only if real shared capital — Musharakah / Mudarabah:** proportional profit *and loss*; no fixed return. Don't force partnership loss-sharing onto a pure commission.
- **The non-negotiable don'ts:** no guaranteed/fixed "return on capital" (riba); no undefined/open-ended fee terms (gharar fahish); the underlying activity must be halal.
- **A Shariah-board sign-off is required before *claiming* "Sharia-compliant"** (and is a marketable asset). Costs are negotiated by complexity/scholar prominence — directional figures only; get a quote.

## PART 4 — BUILD FEASIBILITY (identity resolution + attribution)

### 4.1 Identity resolution — the true critical path (data-quality-bound, not algorithm-bound)

- **Sequencing:** **MVP = deterministic-only** on the cleanest keys (verified email, domain, tax/CR number) + a **human review queue** (confidence-band routing: auto-accept >90%, review 50–90%, reject <50%). **V2 = Splink** (free, gov-grade Fellegi-Sunter + EM, scales to 100M+ records). **V3 = commercial MDM** (Senzing/Tamr/Reltio — opaque six/seven-figure pricing) only if scale/real-time/merge-split governance demands it.
- **The reality that bites:** B2B data decays ~22–30%/yr, and the deterministic keys you most want are often the dirtiest. **The real scaling cost is the human review-queue volume and golden-record survivorship disputes — not the matching math.**
- **De-risk it early (the gate):** on design-partner CRM+billing+marketplace extracts, hand-label a few-hundred-pair gold set, run deterministic + Splink, **plot precision/recall**, set the confidence bands where the curve degrades, and **measure the resulting review-queue volume.** This single experiment discharges the PDR's biggest stated assumption.

### 4.2 Attribution — the PDR's strongest decision, externally validated

- **"Human-authoritative, model-advisory" (ADR-0001) is the single most-validated choice in your corpus** — corroborated by partner-attribution best practice (report **sourced vs. influenced separately**; **dual sign-off** because AEs under-attribute; ~14-day default window) *and* by the **EU AI Act** (credit/financial-impact algorithms are "high-risk," requiring human oversight + traceability + explainability). Auto-credit is both non-compliant and unable to survive disputes. **Contestability is the moat, not a constraint.**
- **Shapley** is the axiomatically-fair gold standard (Efficiency, Symmetry, Null-player, Additivity → unique allocation). It's exponential in general but **exactly computable for the few-partners-per-deal case** — the usual cost objection doesn't apply to you. Good as an *advisory* split (V2).
- **Heuristic MTA** (first/last/linear/time-decay/position) is deprecated (GA4 dropped them in 2023) — advisory baseline only.
- **The genuine research bet — "informativeness-weighted" attribution** (the PDR's distinctive concept): components exist (information-gain/KL, data-driven attribution at LinkedIn/Amazon) but no off-the-shelf implementation matches the concept. It's **data-hungry (~1k–10k conversions/month** early partner programs won't have), correlation-not-causation, and black-box/unstable. **Keep it a V3 spike behind the capture layer**; ship human-authoritative + a simple explainable advisory split first; keep Shapley/heuristic as the defensible fallback.

**Top 3 build risks:** (1) ER is the critical path and is data-quality-bound; (2) informativeness-weighted attribution may not be productizable as conceived; (3) explainability/contestability debt if advisory models drift toward authority.

## PART 6 — THE ORCHESTRATION ENDGAME (category, network, AI, expansion)

### 6.1 Category: own "Partner Revenue / settlement," not "orchestration"

- **"Partnership orchestration" is a vendor/analyst descriptor, not an adoptable buyer category** — many vendors each define it differently (Impartner, ZINFI, Beyond Now, WorkSpan), the signature of a descriptor, not a category. The named, adopted category is **PERM** (Gartner's evolution of PRM) — and it's only a **Market Guide, not a Magic Quadrant**, signaling an immature market already claimed by incumbents.
- **It's also adoption-fatigued:** Forrester (via secondary) — **61% say their ecosystem is "too complex to orchestrate"; only 10–20% of partners drive meaningful revenue.**
- **The ownable slice:** a narrower **"Partner Revenue / settlement"** category — real adjacent budget (RecVue "Partner Settlement," UPM commission/rebate pillars), un-owned by the ELG players (Crossbeam = mapping, WorkSpan = co-sell; neither does money well). Position **adjacent to ELG**: let Crossbeam own "ecosystem-led growth"; you own **"getting partners paid, correctly, across borders, compliantly."**
- **Category-design caution (Play Bigger):** category kings capture ~76% of category *market cap*, but the framework has **survivorship bias** and category creation takes 6–10 years most pre-seed runways can't fund. Practitioner consensus is shifting to **"own a segment, not a category."**

### 6.2 Network-effect design — seed it from Phase 1

The single proof case is **Crossbeam** (account-mapping): ~30,000 companies post-Reveal merger; the igniting mechanic is **privacy-preserving cross-tenant overlap** + **freemium cold-start** (free to connect; pay to route data out). Design implications:
1. **Make cross-tenant identity the wedge, not a feature** — a partner's record reused across all its customers; the asset compounds with each tenant.
2. **Freemium on density, paywall on settlement (money movement)** — copy Crossbeam's seeding economics but monetize the differentiated layer.
3. **Solve cold-start with bilateral utility, not single-player utility** — account-mapping has no single-player value, so seed via a **compliance/settlement job that the larger party values regardless of whether the counterparty is on the network yet** (e.g., a GCC vendor needs ZATCA/WHT/residency-correct reconciliation anyway). That pulls the second side on.
4. **Beware the feature-bag trap** — ship the narrow wedge with deep value before broadening (adoption collapses when partners "jump between ten tabs").
- **Caveat to hold:** "come for the tool, stay for the network" is genuinely contested (Dixon vs. the TechCrunch rebuttal); the inversion "come for the network, stay for the tool" may apply since account-mapping has weak single-player value. This is *why* the bilateral-utility seeding above matters.

### 6.3 Agentic AI — reality check (don't build the narrative on it)

~70% press-release vapor. The one real, dated, shipping product is **WorkSpan's GenAI Co-Sell Agent on the AWS AI Agent Marketplace (July 17, 2025)** — and it does a *narrow* job (recommend best-fit partner, generate the private offer). Crossbeam shipped MCP/AI-chat *retrieval over its data graph*. **The defensible role of AI is on top of a proprietary cross-tenant data asset; an agent with no exclusive data is a commodity wrapper. Build the data/settlement moat first; the agent is a thin, late layer.**

### 6.4 GCC → global expansion

- **The compliance moat is real in-region:** Saudi **PDPL enforced Sept 14, 2024** (cross-border-transfer limits; SAMA/NCA data-residency mandates; hyperscaler local regions live). A compliance-native settlement layer is something an incoming global vendor lacks.
- **Expansion path:** ride the **cloud-marketplace co-sell rail** (AWS/Azure/GCP as neutral global distribution; Omdia $30B→$163B by 2030) and expand into **adjacent high-compliance emerging markets first** (rest of GCC → Egypt/Turkey/SE-Asia/Africa) — **not head-on into the US/EU** where Crossbeam/WorkSpan/Tackle/AppDirect are entrenched.
- **Precedents:** Foodics (genuine MENA→global), Unifonic (emerging-markets-led) — and the cautionary tale, **Kitopi's US entry-then-exit**: MENA-honed regulatory/operational advantages often don't transfer to mature Western markets. Risk: compliance specificity that wins GCC becomes "regional vendor" dead weight elsewhere.
