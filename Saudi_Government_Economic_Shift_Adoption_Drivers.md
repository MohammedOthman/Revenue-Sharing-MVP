## 1. Executive summary — the thesis, deepened

**The one-line bet.** The Saudi state is, by deliberate and capital-backed policy, **re-architecting its own economy into tens of thousands of multi-party, contractual, revenue- and risk-sharing relationships** — and it is doing so *faster than any country has the governance tooling to monitor*. PPP concessions, availability-payment utilities, owner/operator hotel fee splits, local-content set-asides, health-cluster provider/payer settlement, privatized-club ownership webs, esports club-distribution programs, franchise royalties, and Shareek co-investment compacts are **all bilateral-or-multilateral revenue-sharing arrangements that must be attributed, tracked, reconciled, and settled between distinct legal entities** — the exact object Reven is the system of record for. The government is not merely a customer; **it is manufacturing the demand category, and the global evidence (UK, World Bank) is that this category fails without a purpose-built monitoring layer.**

**The six findings that matter most:**

1. **The pivot is irreversible and fiscal.** Non-oil activity is **~55% of real GDP (2025, rebased)**; non-oil revenue hit **49.7% of government income in Q2 2025 — near-parity with oil for the first time.** Accounting for partner-generated revenue is now a sovereign-solvency issue. `[HIGH]`
2. **The PPP machine is built and re-accelerating.** First-ever **PSP Law (M/63, 2021)** + **NCP**; **2018 program: 200+ projects, ~$213bn, ~90 contracts**; **new National Privatization Strategy (Jan 2026): 220+ PPP contracts & $64bn private capital by 2030 across 18 sectors.** Signed PPP concessions hit a **record $28.2bn in 2023 (23%+ of all awarded project value).** `[HIGH]`
3. **The contracts are explicit revenue/risk shares — at named, knowable economics.** Water IWPs are 25-year BOO take-or-pay at tariffs like **SAR 1.55/m³ (Jubail 3A)**; schools PPPs pay **~SAR 86m/yr availability payments**; hotel owner/operator deals run **1.5–4% of gross revenue + 8–10% of gross operating profit**; the Medina airport concession returns a **reported ~54.5% gross-revenue share to government.** `[HIGH on structure]`
4. **SAR 12.4 trillion of co-investment, plus a 12-fund financing stack.** NIS = Shareek **SAR 5tn** + PIF **SAR 3tn** + facilitated **SAR 4tn**; PIF AUM **$913bn → $2.67tn target**; the **National Development Fund** umbrella runs 12 funds (SIDF SAR 105bn, TDF $4bn at ~1:2 blended leverage, Kafalah SAR 13.9bn guarantees, NIF SAR 200bn) whose entire model is co-financing/guaranteeing/revenue-sharing with private partners. `[HIGH/MED]`
5. **Compliance already digitized the attribution data.** ZATCA Phase-2 e-invoicing pulls the **SAR 375k+ turnover base** into cleared XML; **WHT (royalties 15%, management fees 20%) + RHQ 0%-tax status** make cross-border partner payouts a regulated act; **Etimad** carries **SAR 2tn** of government disbursement; statutory rails (**Nphies, Najm, Wafi, Mudad**) are ready-made revenue oracles. `[HIGH]`
6. **There is a governance gap and a white space.** Global best practice now includes purpose-built PPP-monitoring software (**World Bank 3P-CMT**), yet even the **UK NAO (2025)** finds authorities "lack up-to-date contracts/asset registers"; KSA targeted searches surface **no purpose-built government/semi-gov revenue-share system of record** — the function runs on ERP modules, SI-built systems, and spreadsheets. `[HIGH on the benchmark; MED on the KSA white-space (absence-of-evidence)]`

## 3. Shift 2 — The PPP & privatization machine (new policy architecture)

The Kingdom built, from scratch, the legal and institutional plumbing to convert public assets and services into long-dated, multi-party, revenue-sharing contracts — and has just re-accelerated it.

**The legal & institutional foundation.**
- **PSP Law — the first formal PPP/privatization statute.** Royal Decree **M/63** (with CoM Resolution No. 436), **18 March 2021**; **in force 24 July 2021** (120 days post-publication). Governs PPP *and* divestment; defines a PPP as a ≥5-year infrastructure/public-service contract in which the private party performs ≥2 of design/build/manage/operate/maintain/finance; **permits arbitration (including offshore-seated)**; and **entitles the private party to compensation for loss of earnings on a no-fault government termination.** `[HIGH]` — [Norton Rose](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en/knowledge/publications/0cb2c59a/the-new-saudi-arabian-private-sector-participation-law-top-ten-considerations); [Clifford Chance](https://www.cliffordchance.com/briefings/2021/04/client-alerter--issuance-of-private-sector-participation-law.html); [Dentons](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/articles/2021/march/17/issuance-of-the-private-sector-participation-law-in-saudi-arabia).
- **Implementing regulations** gazetted **7 January 2022**. `[HIGH]` — [Gowling WLG](https://gowlingwlg.com/en/insights-resources/articles/2022/saudi-arabia-approves-psp-law-implementing-regs).
- **National Center for Privatization & PPP (NCP)** — **CoM Resolution No. 355, 3 April 2017**, linked to CEDA; plans, structures and executes transactions. `[HIGH]` — [Saudipedia](https://saudipedia.com/en/national-center-for-privatization-ppp).

**Targets & achieved.**
- Privatization Program goal: private-sector GDP **40% → 65%**. `[TARGET, HIGH]`
- **2018 program at conclusion: >200 projects approved, ~$213bn total investment, ~90 contracts signed.** `[ACTUAL, MED–HIGH]` — [PR Newswire, 29 Jan 2026](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/saudi-arabia-unveils-ambitious-new-privatization-strategy-to-expand-public-private-partnerships-302673965.html).
- **2023 record: $28.2bn of signed PPP concessions = 23%+ of the total value of all awarded project contracts** (vs **3.5% in 2018**; 18.3% in 2024). `[ACTUAL, HIGH]` — [MEED](https://www.meed.com/rise-in-ppps-reflects-saudi-budgetary-pragmatism).

**The 2026 re-acceleration — the freshest, strongest signal.**
- **29 January 2026: new National Privatization Strategy — 220+ PPP contracts by 2030, $64bn (SAR 240bn) private capital, 18 sectors** (adds transport & logistics, water, health & education infrastructure, real estate), explicitly **transitioning from a "foundational" to an "execution/delivery" phase.** `[HIGH]` — [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/saudi-arabia-unveils-ambitious-new-privatization-strategy-to-expand-public-private-partnerships-302673974.html); [AGBI](https://www.agbi.com/analysis/construction/2026/02/saudi-arabia-widens-ppp-scope-to-meet-infrastructure-demands/).
- **Total pipeline near 300 projects** (98 published + ~200 pending, FII); KSA **2nd in the emerging-markets PPP pipeline.** `[MED]` — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2633786/business-economy).

> **→ Reven implication.** A PPP/concession is the **canonical Reven object at sovereign scale**: ≥2 legal entities, a decades-long contract, performance/availability-linked payments, no-fault-termination compensation, and a need for a neutral, auditable ledger of *what each party is owed and why*. 220+ new such contracts and $64bn of fresh private capital, on a published 2030 schedule, is a **named, dated, capital-backed pipeline of revenue-sharing relationships.**

## 4. How Saudi PPP / revenue-sharing contracts actually work (the mechanics)

This is where the thesis stops being macro and becomes a product spec. Every structure below is a *named, real* revenue/risk split that a system of record must compute and reconcile.

### 4.1 Contract typologies & who bears the risk
KSA PPPs are typically **20–30-year concessions**, with government preferring **availability-based payments** for social infrastructure (to remove demand risk from investors) and **take-or-pay off-take** for utilities. `[MED]` — [Aninver](https://aninver.com/blog/public-private-partnerships-in-saudi-arabia-trends-frameworks-and-investment-opportunities). The spectrum: **DBFO/DBFOT, BOO, BOOT/BTO, O&M, management contracts**, ranging from government-pays-availability to merchant/demand-risk to explicit gross-revenue-share concessions.

### 4.2 Water/desalination — the BOO take-or-pay machine (SWPC)
The Saudi Water Partnership Company (SWPC) is the **sole government-owned off-taker** buying 100% of plant output under a **25-year Water Purchase Agreement on a capacity-and-output payment mechanism**, with the **Minister of Finance providing a sovereign credit-support agreement** backstopping SWPC. `[MED–HIGH]` — [DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2021/07/yanbu-4-iwp-and-its-transmission-pipe); [World Construction Network](https://www.worldconstructionnetwork.com/sponsored/qa-with-saudi-water-partnership-company-swpc-attracting-global-ppp-developers-to-the-kingdoms-water-future/). ### 4.3 Airports — explicit gross-revenue share to government
**Medina (Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz Intl) was KSA's first aviation PPP**: a **25-year Build-Transfer-Operate** concession with **TIBAH** (TAV + Al Rajhi + Saudi Oger), **$1.2bn financial close June 2012** (first fully Islamic-financed airport). The concession features **a reported ~54.5% gross-revenue share back to GACA** — a textbook concession-fee/revenue-share. `[HIGH on structure; LOW on the % — single-source]` — [IJGlobal](https://www.ijglobal.com/articles/79447/medina-airport-ppp-saudi-arabia); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Mohammad_bin_Abdulaziz_International_Airport). The current **Matarat/NCP wave (Taif, Abha, Hail, Qassim) uses 30-year BTOs**; Matarat Holding operates all **29 civilian airports.** `[MED]` — [MEED](https://www.meed.com/saudi-arabia-seeks-taif-airport-ppp-interest).

### 4.4 Social infrastructure — availability payments you can underwrite
- **Schools PPP (Tatweer Building Co. + Ministry of Education):** program authorized at **≤SAR 400m/yr for ≤28 years to deliver ≥120 schools.** **Wave 1 (60 schools, Jeddah/Makkah, >50,000 students)** — KSA's first social-infrastructure PPP — reached **financial close April 2021**, structured **Build-Maintain-Transfer** (3-yr build + 20-yr maintenance) against a government **Annual Equivalent (availability) Payment of ~SAR 86m**; sponsors Vision Invest/Al Mutlaq/Omran Al Omran/Al Bawani. **Wave 2** added 60 schools in Madinah. `[HIGH]` — [Tatweer Building Co.](https://tbc.sa/EN/News-single/e/187); [Zawya](https://www.zawya.com/en/press-release/wave-1of-schools-ppp-reaches-financial-close-rfvwd9vq).
- **Healthcare — first PPP:** a **diagnostic-imaging concession (June 2023)** to **Altakassusi Alliance Medical** (Alliance Medical + Nexus Gulf + KFSH Intl Holding), a **10-year concession** operating radiology/nuclear-medicine across **7 Riyadh-cluster hospitals (>1m beneficiaries)**, **financial close Oct 2024 with IFC support.** `[HIGH]` — [IFC](https://www.ifc.org/en/pressroom/2023/ifc-supported-saudi-healthcare-ppp-awarded-to-leading-diagnostic-imaging-company).
- **Waste-to-energy/circular economy:** >$2bn of BOT closed since 2024; a Riyadh WtE plant (~1.3m t/yr, ~$800m, ~200 MW by 2029) planned; sector opportunity ~SAR 420bn by 2040. `[MED, forward/target]` — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2626338/business-economy).

> **→ Reven implication.** These are not abstractions: a 25-year take-or-pay at SAR 1.55/m³, a SAR 86m annual availability payment, a 54.5% airport revenue-share, a 10-year imaging concession across 7 hospitals — each is a **multi-party payment rule + performance condition + reconciliation obligation over decades.** That is precisely the eligibility/ledger/reconciliation engine Reven's Settle phase is built to run.

## 10. The governance gap — why a system of record (global benchmark)

This is the ROI backbone. The world's PPP programs prove that multi-party concession revenue **erodes and leaks without a purpose-built monitoring layer** — and that such software is now codified best practice.

- **It fails without monitoring (UK).** The **NAO (2018)** found **>700 operational PFI/PF2 contracts, £57bn capital value**, but **£199bn of future charges to the 2040s** — and concluded extra costs "for no clear benefit." The **NAO (2025) "Lessons learned"** (140+ reports) found authorities **lack up-to-date contracts, asset-condition data, or clear asset registers** as contracts expire — a systemic system-of-record failure. `[HIGH]` — [NAO 2018](https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/PFI-and-PF2.pdf); [NAO 2025](https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/lessons-learned-private-finance-for-infrastructure-summary.pdf).
- **Revenue erodes fast (World Bank / Guasch).** Across **1,000+ infrastructure concessions**, **~30% were renegotiated, on average 2.2 years after award** — **55% in transport, 75% in water** — usually under information asymmetry favouring the operator; toll-road studies show **mismatches between system-recorded and reported collections** (manual reconciliation → leakage). `[HIGH on renegotiation; MED on leakage]` — [Guasch (World Bank, 2004)](https://regulationbodyofknowledge.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Guasch_Granting_and_Renegotiating.pdf).
- **Best practice = a dedicated monitoring system.** The **World Bank PPP Reference Guide v3** requires whole-life contract monitoring; and the World Bank publishes a **purpose-built "Contract Management and PPP Portfolio Monitoring System (3P-CMT)"** to "help contracting authorities monitor implementation of PPP projects and associated risks more effectively." **Australia's Partnerships Victoria** uses the Treasury Corporation to model payments and verify the abatement regime; **India's PPPAC** standardizes Model Concession Agreements and Viability Gap Funding. `[HIGH]` — [World Bank 3P-CMT User Guide](https://documents.worldbank.org/en/publication/documents-reports/documentdetail/099647512222211698/p17425408007730a70a1e90b1b6c75b36d4); [PPP Reference Guide v3](https://ppp.worldbank.org/sites/default/files/2024-08/PPP%20Reference%20Guide%20Version%203.pdf).

> **→ Reven implication.** KSA is signing decades-long, multi-party contracts **faster than almost anyone — and faster than it has built the governance tooling to monitor them.** The global record says this is precisely where revenue erodes and leaks. Reven *is* the 3P-CMT-grade layer (claim ledger → eligibility → reconciliation → settlement) for the partner/concession economy — and the **leakage benchmark (3–8% of payouts; renegotiation within ~2 years) is the hard-dollar ROI a CFO can underwrite.**

## 12. Synthesis — mapping every shift onto Reven's phase model
