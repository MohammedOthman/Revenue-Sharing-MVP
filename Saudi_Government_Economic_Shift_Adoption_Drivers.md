## 1. Executive summary — the thesis in one page

**The one-line bet.** The Saudi state is, by deliberate policy, **re-architecting its own economy into thousands of multi-party, contractual, revenue- and risk-sharing relationships** — PPP concessions, availability-payment utilities, local-content set-asides, giga-project management agreements, health-cluster provider/payer settlement, privatized-club ownership webs, and Shareek co-investment compacts. **Every one of these is, structurally, a bilateral-or-multilateral revenue-sharing arrangement that must be attributed, tracked, reconciled, and settled between distinct legal entities** — which is exactly the system-of-record Reven is built to be. The government is not just a customer; **it is manufacturing the demand category.**

**The five shifts that prove the tailwind (each detailed below, each sourced):**

## 3. Shift 2 — The PPP & privatization machine (new policy architecture)

The Kingdom built, from scratch, the legal and institutional plumbing to convert public assets and services into long-dated, multi-party, revenue-sharing contracts — and has just re-accelerated it.

### 3.1 The legal & institutional foundation

- **PSP Law — the first formal PPP/privatization statute.** Promulgated by **Royal Decree M/63** (with CoM Resolution No. 436), **18 March 2021**; **in force 24 July 2021**. It governs PPP *and* divestment, defines a PPP as a ≥5-year infrastructure/public-service contract in which the private party performs ≥2 of design/build/manage/operate/maintain/finance, and — critically for investors — **permits arbitration of disputes**. **[HIGH]** — [Norton Rose Fulbright](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en/knowledge/publications/0cb2c59a/the-new-saudi-arabian-private-sector-participation-law-top-ten-considerations); [Dentons](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/articles/2021/march/17/issuance-of-the-private-sector-participation-law-in-saudi-arabia); [Clifford Chance](https://www.cliffordchance.com/briefings/2021/04/client-alerter--issuance-of-private-sector-participation-law.html).
- **Implementing regulations** gazetted **7 January 2022**, replacing the older Privatization Projects Manual. **[HIGH]** — [Gowling WLG](https://gowlingwlg.com/en/insights-resources/articles/2022/saudi-arabia-approves-psp-law-implementing-regs).
- **The National Center for Privatization & PPP (NCP)** — established by **CoM Resolution No. 355, 3 April 2017**, linked to CEDA with financial/administrative independence; plans, structures and executes privatization and PPP transactions across the economy. **[HIGH]** — [Saudipedia](https://saudipedia.com/en/national-center-for-privatization-ppp).

### 3.2 The Privatization Program (Vision 2030 VRP) — targets & achieved

### 3.3 The deal flow is real and record-setting

- **2023 was a record year for signed PPP concessions: $28.2bn — more than 23% of the total value of *all* project contracts awarded that year** (vs a 2019–22 average of 15.6% and just **3.5% in 2018**); the share eased to 18.3% in 2024 but the structural step-change is unmistakable. **[HIGH]** — [MEED](https://www.meed.com/rise-in-ppps-reflects-saudi-budgetary-pragmatism).
- **Water sector** shows the model concretely: **40+ water PPPs**; new desalination plants are **Build-Own-Operate (BOO) on ~25-year concessions** (IWPs) and sewage plants BOOT, paid on an **availability/performance basis against a sovereign-backed off-take (SWPC)** — i.e. a structured, multi-year revenue-share. **SWPC has attracted >$12bn of private investment.** Ras Mohaisen IWP reached financial close at **SAR 2.57bn** (ACWA Power 45% / HACO 35% / AlKifah 20%). **[MED–HIGH]** — [Aninver](https://aninver.com/blog/public-private-partnerships-in-saudi-arabia-trends-frameworks-and-investment-opportunities); [Smart Water Magazine](https://smartwatermagazine.com/news/saudi-water-partnership-company/swpc-reaches-financial-close-us686-million-ras-mohaisen).

### 3.4 The 2026 re-acceleration — the freshest, strongest signal

- **On 29 January 2026 the Kingdom launched a new National Privatization Strategy** targeting **more than 220 PPP contracts by 2030** and the mobilization of **over $64bn (SAR 240bn) in private capital**, across **18 sectors** (adding transport & logistics, water, health & education infrastructure, and real estate), explicitly framed as the transition **from a "foundational" to an "execution/delivery" phase.** **[HIGH]** — [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/saudi-arabia-unveils-ambitious-new-privatization-strategy-to-expand-public-private-partnerships-302673974.html); [AGBI](https://www.agbi.com/analysis/construction/2026/02/saudi-arabia-widens-ppp-scope-to-meet-infrastructure-demands/); [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2633786/business-economy).
- Early-2026 reporting (FII) puts the **total pipeline near 300 projects** (98 published/announced + ~200 awaiting approval) and ranks KSA **second in the emerging-markets PPP pipeline** within a GCC project pipeline exceeding $2.5tn. **[MED]** — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2633786/business-economy).

> **→ Reven implication.** A PPP/concession is **the canonical Reven object at sovereign scale**: two-or-more legal entities, a long-dated contract, performance/availability-linked payments, and a need for a neutral, auditable ledger of *what each party is owed and why*. 220+ new such contracts and $64bn of fresh private capital, on a published 2030 schedule, is a **named, dated, capital-backed pipeline of revenue-sharing relationships** — the Orchestrate-phase TAM, being built by the state itself.

## 4. Shift 3 — SAR 12.4 trillion of orchestrated co-investment (NIS · PIF · Shareek)

The Kingdom explicitly **aggregates** government, sovereign-fund, private-champion and foreign capital into one coordinated plan — i.e. it is *institutionalising* multi-party co-investment, the JV-grade cousin of revenue-sharing.

### 4.1 The National Investment Strategy (NIS, 2021)

- **Cumulative investment target: SAR 12.4 trillion by 2030**, decomposed as **Shareek SAR 5tn + PIF SAR 3tn + NIS-facilitated SAR 4tn**; investment-to-GDP **22% → 30%**. **[MED]** (aggregator-sourced; primary-confirm) — [vision2030.ai](https://vision2030.ai/encyclopedia/national-investment-strategy/); [Saudipedia](https://saudipedia.com/en/national-investment-strategy).
- The NIS **exceeded its 2024 targets for a fourth consecutive year** across gross fixed capital formation, FDI and RHQ relocations; **GFCF reached SAR 1.33tn (2024, +4.5%)**, ~89% of it private-sector. **[MED–HIGH]** — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2599738/business-economy).

And **each privatization spins up a *new* legal entity with new partner/commercial relationships from day one** (the airport operator's concession revenue, the club's sponsorship/broadcast splits, the miller's distributor network) — greenfield Reven accounts with no legacy system to displace.

## 6. Shift 5 — Whole sectors re-platformed as multi-stakeholder networks

Each flagship sector is being deliberately re-organised into a **many-to-many** structure of distinct entities that share revenue, risk and payment — the native habitat for a revenue-sharing system of record.

### 6.1 Healthcare — the cleanest provider/payer/regulator split in the region
- The MoH is being **unbundled into three roles**: **regulator (MoH)**, **provider (Health Holding Company, est. 2022)**, **payer (Council of Health Insurance / Center for National Health Insurance)** — with **~21 health clusters** and **~290 hospitals + 2,300 primary-care centres** corporatized toward accountable-care organisations. **[HIGH]** — [PMC qualitative study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11103359/); [trade.gov healthcare](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/saudi-arabia-healthcare).
- HHC named **nine PPP priority areas**; MoH expects **>100 PPP projects and $12.8bn of private investment over five years**, within **>$65bn of healthcare infrastructure spend.** **[MED–HIGH]** — [trade.gov healthcare](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/saudi-arabia-healthcare).
- Mandatory cooperative insurance covers **12m+ residents**; **domestic-worker insurance** became mandatory **1 July 2024**; the unified **Daman** program is consolidating the framework. **[MED–HIGH]** — [P4H/PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11103359/).
- *Reven hook:* a payer (CHI) strategically **purchasing services from provider clusters under performance contracts** is a recurring, adjudicated, multi-party settlement flow — with **Nphies** (the national claims rail) as a ready-made revenue oracle.

### 6.2 Tourism — a partner-dense, fast-overshooting sector
- **122m visitors in 2025 (~SR300bn spend, +6%)**; the **original 100m target was beaten in 2023, six years early**; the **new target is 150m by 2030** (70m international + 80m domestic). Tourism-GDP target **3% → 10%**, ~1.6m jobs. **[HIGH]** — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2630117/business-economy); [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/10/30/saudi-arabia-tourism-vision-2030/).
- *Reven hook:* tourism runs on **operator/owner/OTA/agent revenue splits** — a high-volume, multi-party commission web.

### 6.3 Sports, entertainment & events — newly commercial, newly multi-owner
- **Football-club privatization (June 2023)** created **ownership webs** (PIF, Aramco, Diriyah, RCU, NEOM as owners; foundations as minority holders) atop sponsorship/broadcast/transfer revenue splits; **first foreign club ownership** (Al-Kholood → Harburg Group, July 2025). **[HIGH]** — [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/6/5/saudi-arabia-privatises-football-clubs-eyes-big-name-signings); [SportsPro](https://www.sportspro.com/news/saudi-pro-league-privatisation-foreign-owners-investment-al-kholood-harburg-july-2025/).
- **General Entertainment Authority** anchors a **~$64bn decade-long** entertainment-investment program; **Riyadh Season 2024 drew ~13m visitors**; the **Esports World Cup** (2025 prize pool ~$70m) sits on **Savvy Games' ~$38bn** commitment toward a **SAR 50bn gaming-GDP target.** **[MED–HIGH]** — [Wikipedia/GEA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Entertainment_Authority); [Arabian Business](https://www.arabianbusiness.com/).

### 6.4 Giga-projects — management agreements *are* revenue-sharing contracts
- **Contract awards across giga-projects rose ~20% to $196bn in 2025** (Knight Frank); 17 western giga-projects carry **$431.3bn announced investment** ($57bn awarded, $187.2bn pipeline). **[HIGH]** — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2617929/business-economy); [The National](https://www.thenationalnews.com/business/2025/10/06/contract-value-of-saudi-giga-projects-jumps-20-to-196-billion/).
- **Red Sea Global** signed **hotel-management agreements with Marriott, Accor, Hyatt, IHG and Jumeirah** to operate **9 of 16 Phase-One properties** — i.e. **owner/operator revenue-share contracts** (management fees + performance shares). **[MED–HIGH]** — [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2089811/business-economy).
- **ROSHN** runs a **master-developer land-sale / partnership model** with local developers (e.g. SAR 1.5bn construction + SAR 448m land-sale agreements), explicitly framed at the **PIF Private Sector Forum** around local-content commitments. **Qiddiya** awarded **~$10bn** of contracts (incl. a $53.2m two-club partnership) and merged with **SEVEN**; **Diriyah** awarded $5.9bn (2024) + $3.7bn (2025). **[MED]** — [ROSHN](https://www.roshn.sa/news-and-events/press-releases/article-41); [SUSTG](https://www.sustg.com/qiddiya-awards-2-6-billion-in-contracts-to-speed-up-development-diriyah-gate-full-speed-ahead/).

### 6.5 Digital, fintech & the SME long tail — the rails and the buyers
- **Digital economy = 16.0% of GDP (2024, GASTAT)**; **fintechs reached ~261 (end-2025)** toward a **525-by-2030 target**; **Open Banking** live (AIS 2022, **Payment-Initiation Services Sept 2024**); **e-payments ~79% of retail (2024)**, beating target early; **8 unicorns.** **[HIGH/MED]** — [GASTAT news/150](https://www.stats.gov.sa/en/w/news/150); [SPA N2185748](https://www.spa.gov.sa/en/N2185748); trade.gov.
- **VC:** KSA **led MENA in 2024 (~$750m / 178 deals)** and raised **$860m in H1 2025 (+116%)**, fintech funding +275%. **Biban 2025 generated >$6bn** of SME deals. **[MED–HIGH]** — [MAGNiTT/Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2585680/business-economy).
- **Monsha'at:** SME GDP-share target **20% → 35%**; **procurement set-asides** for SMEs/SOEs; **813,000+ registered SMEs (2023, +108% since 2016).** **[MED]** — [Vision 2030/Monsha'at](https://www.vision2030.gov.sa/en/explore/explore-more/monshaat).

> **→ Reven implication.** Health clusters (provider/payer settlement), tourism (operator/agent commissions), privatized clubs (ownership + sponsorship/broadcast splits), giga-project management agreements (owner/operator fee-and-performance shares), and open-banking rails are **all multi-stakeholder revenue-sharing webs** — each a Reven account, each backed by a government/sector rail (Nphies, Najm, Etimad, sarie) that can serve as the revenue oracle without per-customer integration.

## 7. Synthesis — mapping each shift onto Reven's phase model

Reven's arc is **Capture (PRM) → Settle (revenue system-of-record) → Orchestrate (partnership network)** (`README.md`). The Saudi shifts feed each phase:

| Reven phase | What it needs to exist | The Saudi shift that supplies it |
|---|---|---|
| **Capture** (claim ledger, attribution) | Many firms running material partner programs; a digital revenue/contribution record | **Local-content mandates + Etimad** (per-contract supplier attribution, already digital); **Shareek/IKTVA** localization tracking; the **non-oil pivot** making partner-attributed revenue a priority to record |
| **Settle** (rule engine, ledger, reconciliation, compliance) | Multi-party contracts with eligibility/payment rules and audit needs | **PPP/concession contracts** (availability/performance payments); **health provider/payer settlement**; **VAT/WHT/ZATCA** compliance turning settlement into a regulated act |
| **Orchestrate** (partner P&L, cross-entity network) | A dense web of inter-entity revenue relationships at scale | **SAR 12.4tn co-investment** (state/PIF/champion JVs); **giga-project owner/operator agreements**; **220+ new PPPs by 2030**; **privatized-club ownership webs** |

**The causal claim, stated plainly and defensibly:** Reven does not merely *operate in* a growing market — **the Saudi state is, on a published and capital-backed timetable, converting public assets and services into the exact multi-party revenue-sharing structures Reven is the system of record for.** The non-oil pivot makes accounting for those flows a fiscal priority; the PPP machine and privatization manufacture the contracts; the NIS/PIF/Shareek capital scales them; local-content rules make per-party attribution a legal duty; and sector re-platforming spreads the pattern across health, tourism, sport, real estate and finance. That is a **demand-creation engine owned by the customer's own government.**

**Where this dossier extends the existing value-pool doc.** `Saudi_Value_Pool_and_ICP_Strategic_Analysis.md` sized the *software/compliance* pool (ZATCA, RHQ, MISA) and priced the ICP. This dossier supplies the **upstream causal layer** — *why the multi-party relationships that fill that pool are being created in the first place* — and surfaces **two additional government rails** (the **PPP/concession pipeline** as the Orchestrate-phase TAM, and **LCGPA/Etimad local content** as a second compliance-grade attribution wedge alongside ZATCA).
