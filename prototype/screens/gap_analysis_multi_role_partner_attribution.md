# Gap Analysis: Multi-Role Partner Attribution

## 1. Current State (The "Sourcing" Bias)
The current OS is optimized for "Deal Source" (Sourced/Referral). While "Influenced" and "Multi-Partner Splits" exist, the platform lacks a dedicated framework for non-sourcing partners who are critical for deal closure and delivery success.

## 2. Identified Gaps
*   **Role Identification Gap**: Missing granular classifications for "Implementation Partner," "Service Delivery Partner," and "Integration Architect" roles during the attribution phase.
*   **Contribution Evidence Gap**: Non-sourcing partners don't submit "Leads"; they submit "Service Milestones," "Architectural Sign-offs," or "Deployment Logs."
*   **Economic Model Gap**: Commission-only logic doesn't work for delivery partners who might receive "Implementation Credits," "Service Rebates," or "SLA Bonuses" instead of a % of contract value.
*   **Visibility Gap**: Sourcing partners often don't see who is "delivering" their deal, leading to operational friction.

## 3. Functional Requirements for Scalability
*   **Multi-Role Claim Ledger**: Allow multiple partners to attach to a single Opportunity with distinct "Influence Types."
*   **Service Evidence Vault**: A place to upload proof of delivery or integration work as valid claim evidence.
*   **Attribute-to-Outcome Mapping**: Linking partner "Services" (e.g., successful API integration) to "Revenue Realization" (e.g., the customer paying the bill).
*   **Unified Partner P&L (Service-Aware)**: Calculating ROI not just on sales commission, but on the cost of delivery partners vs. the LTV increase they drive.

## 4. Implementation Plan
*   **New Screen**: "Advanced Multi-Partner Attribution Hub" to manage complex deal ecosystems.
*   **Update Screen**: Enhance "Partner Profile" to reflect "Service Certifications" and "Delivery Track Record."
*   **Update Screen**: "Agreement Rulebook" to include "Service-Based Incentives."
